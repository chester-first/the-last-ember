# THE LAST EMBER — Session Zero Kit
### A GM runsheet + a player-facing handout for the bonding

This file has two halves:
- **Part 1 (GM):** how to run the session-zero table — the pitch, safety, character hooks, and
  the clocks.
- **Part 2 (player handout):** print or share this verbatim. It is written to the players,
  stays in-world where that helps, and walks them through choosing and bonding their legacy
  item.

---
# PART 1 — GM RUNSHEET

### Order of the night
1. **Pitch & tone** (5 min). Read the one-liner and set the heroic-dark-fantasy dial.
2. **Safety tools** (10 min). Set up lines & veils and an X-card, and name the heavy themes out
   loud: undeath, grief, "is death good?", the possibility of permanent character death, and an
   NPC you'll love who may be lost. Get everyone's buy-in.
3. **The expectation** (2 min). Say this plainly: "Death is part of this story. Coming back is
   never free. That's the game we're playing."
4. **Characters & hooks** (30 min). Build the PCs and assign each one a **Marrowmoor tie**
   (below).
5. **The three clocks** (5 min). Show players the **Ember Clock** openly — 12 segments, and it
   only ever drains. Keep the **Wardens meter** and **Sera's Tether** behind the screen.
6. **The bonding** (20 min). Run Part 2 with the players, and have each one pick and describe an
   item. It formally wakes in the first session at the barrow-altar, but they should pick it
   tonight.

### The pitch (read aloud)
> "Three generations ago, the world started running low on life. Fewer children born. Springs
> that don't quite come. And the dead — the dead don't always stay down. They call it the
> Dimming, and everyone blames everything except the truth. The truth is a thousand miles away,
> golden and grieving, and it used to be the thing that *guarded* us. You start as nobodies in
> a fog-bound borderland where the dead have begun walking home to their own front doors. By
> the end, you'll stand at the source of every soul that ever was — and decide whether death
> gets to keep being real."

### Marrowmoor ties (give each PC one to root them in Tier I)
Roll or pick. Each one ties a PC to **Lantern's Rest**, the starting town:
1. **Born here.** One of your own dead has come walking home, and you haven't gone to look yet.
2. **Newcomer with a debt.** You owe the innkeep, Mother Ostry, and she's calling it in.
3. **Warden-blooded.** A grandparent wore the gray of the Emberwardens, and you carry their
   token. This PC should strongly consider the *Soul-Lantern* or *Wardens' Aegis* item.
4. **Grave-trade.** You dig, mourn, doctor, or undertake — you handle the dead for a living, and
   lately the job fights back.
5. **Passing through.** You meant to leave yesterday, but the fog won't let the road go.
6. **Looking for someone.** A person you love came to the Marrowmoor and never wrote again.

### The clocks, set tonight
- **Ember Clock:** 12 / 12. Visible. Tell players: *"Every soul this world loses, you'll watch
  this drop. You can slow it. You can't refill it until the very end — if you earn the right."*
- **Wardens meter:** 0 (hidden). It runs from Keepers (+) to Tallymasters (−).
- **Sera's Tether:** N/A yet — she's met in the first dungeon at **7 / 10**.

# PART 2 — PLAYER HANDOUT
*(Share this with your players. It speaks to them directly.)*

## What you need to know about the world

You live in **Aurevel**, an ordinary enough place of walled towns, old roads, and older
kingdoms. But for as long as your grandparents can remember, something has been wrong. They call
it the **Dimming**: fewer babies, thinner harvests, and hearths that won't hold their heat. And
sometimes the dead get up and try to go home.

Priests teach one thing as bedrock truth: every living soul carries a mote of a single great
fire, the **Ember**. When you die, your mote goes back to **the Source** to be kindled into a new
life. That's how the world keeps making more of itself, and that's supposed to be how it works.

You don't know it yet, but the Dimming is a theft. Someone is hoarding the dead, keeping their
motes instead of letting them return, and the world is slowly running out of life to be born.
Every walking corpse you'll meet is a soul that won't go home.

You start as nobodies in **the Marrowmoor**, a fog-soaked borderland, in the town of **Lantern's
Rest**, where the dead have just begun walking back to their own doorsteps.

## Your bonded item — the most important choice you'll make tonight

In the Marrowmoor there is a barrow-altar where the old guardians once forged soul into iron. In
your first session, each of you will lay an object on that altar, and it will drink a mote of
your own fire and wake up. From then on it's yours, and it grows as you do, all the way to 20th
level. It can't be stolen or broken while you live, and it comes back to your hand after every
long rest.

Tonight, pick that object. It should be something your character would actually carry and care
about. A few prompts:

- A **weapon** handed down to you, or one you made yourself.
- A **holy symbol**, prayer-beads, or a relic.
- An **heirloom** such as a ring, a locket, a cloak-pin, or your grandparent's Warden token.
- An **instrument**, a deck of cards, a surgeon's kit, or a hammer.
- A **shield** or a piece of armor you've never fought without.

Then answer three questions about it, a sentence each:
1. Where did it come from, and who had it before you?
2. What does it mean to you that you'd bleed a piece of your own soul into it?
3. What does it look like when it's angry, or grieving? (Items here have moods.)

Your GM will help you turn it into one of six kinds of legacy item: a blade, a shield, a holy
lantern, a song, a dragon-token, or a quiet heirloom. You don't need the mechanics tonight. You
just need to know what it is and why it matters.

## One thing to brace for

This is a story where death is real and coming back is never free. Your characters can die, and
the world can lose. You will meet someone — a knight named Sera — who is already half-gone, and
whether you can save her depends on how you treat her. That's not us threatening you; that's the
game. Heroes, real stakes, and real mercy that costs something. Bring a character you'd be proud
to lose well.

## A warning you'll hear in-world, so hear it now
The thing hoarding the dead doesn't think it's the villain. It thinks it's saving everyone from
having to die at all. Somewhere out there, it's grieving. Keep that in your pocket. You'll need
it.
