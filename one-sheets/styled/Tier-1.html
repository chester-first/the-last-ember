Tier I — The Marrowmoor

Material Plane · Levels 1–4 · ~4–6 sessions
“the dead won't rest.”

The party are nobodies in a fog-bound borderland where the dead have begun walking home to their own front doors. They bond their soul-forged items, free the revenant knight Sera, stop the Herald's anchoring rite — and, for one un-fightable moment, meet the grieving god behind it all. Full detail: Tier-1-The-Marrowmoor.md.

Must Happen

  1. Bond the legacy items at the Barrow-Altar (R3). Items wake — Kindled tier.
  2. Free (or fail) Sera at R7. Revenant ally; Tether starts 7/10.
  3. Witness Halthurax alive (R9b) — a scene, NOT a fight.

The Herald

medium humanoid (death-priest), CR 3 · the Goldwyrm's agent
Armor Class 14   Hit Points 78   Speed 30 ft.
Touch of the Hoard. Melee spell attack: +5, 3d6 necrotic, and the target's hit point maximum drops by that amount until a long rest.
Gray Sermon (Recharge 5–6). Each enemy within 30 ft: DC 13 Wis save or frightened + 2d8 psychic. A PC who read the Warden Creed (R5) has advantage.
Reinforce the Anchor. Regains 10 HP at the start of each turn — UNLESS the party emptied the R8 Hoard-Cells. ← reward mercy.
Minions. 2× Specter + 4× Skeleton rise from the anchor on initiative 20.

Clocks This Tier

  • Ember 12 → 11 if the party scatters 4+ motes for speed. Show the choice on every undead.
  • Court meter ±1 with Warden Greel (heed him = +, mock/burn = −).
  • Sera's Tether starts at 7.

The Mercy Choice

Every undead = a stolen soul. Return (action + DC 10) sends it home and slows the Ember; Scatter (just kill) is fast but lost forever. The doorstep husband Edon and the Mourn-house child are the tutorials — make them sad, not scary.

The mirror shows an eye, gold and ancient and big as a cathedral door… “Go home, little embers. Grow. I can wait.”

Run R9b as awe, not a TPK. Halthurax (CR 17) will not fight; if attacked he restrains, lectures, and leaves.

Loot

End of Tier

Level 4; legacy items hit Burning entering Tier II. Sera feels a pull “toward the deep woods” = the fey crossing.
