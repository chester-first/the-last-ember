# Tier IV — The Emberthrone
##### The Source · Levels 17–20 · ~5–8 sessions · *"he is replacing death itself."* · **Death is expected; so is grace.**

The party climb into a hollow heaven built room by room around a single failing flame, unmaking the last hoard means releasing souls dead a thousand years, and the Becoming God offers them a genuine bargain they actually can take. They face Halthurax in his finale form — won by emptying his Hoard Pool, not just his HP — and choose which way death itself will end.

## Must Happen
1. **The Choir** — unmaking the last hoard means **releasing souls dead 1,000 years** (incl. the child, Mother Cinder).
2. **The thesis scene** — Halthurax's **genuine** bargain. **The party CAN take it.**
3. **The finale** — won by **emptying his Hoard Pool (180)**, not just HP.

## The Becoming God
*Gargantuan undead deity-thing, eff. CR 26 — 3 phases · Halthurax, Form IV*
___
**Armor Class** :: 22
**Hit Points**  :: 560
**Hoard Pool**  :: 180

***Hoard Pool 180.*** He **can't drop below 1 HP while Pool > 0.** Drain via Choir-anchors (≈20), releases (DC 20 ≈10), or **capstone items.**
:
***P1 (180–121).*** The Choir grants him *advantage on saves.*
:
***P2 (120–61).*** False-dawn breath + **Unmake** (DC 23 Con or drop to 0).
:
***P3 (60–0).*** Pleading; the thesis can interrupt; **Pool 0 + 0 HP = permanent death.**
:
***Capstones answer Unmake.*** Aegis *cancel a death* · Pyre-Blade *catch a falling ally* · Soul-Lantern *path to the Source.*

## Conflict Payoffs
**Court:** redeemed → **army of First Light** breaches with you (no adds harrying the finale). Corrupted → Tallymasters fight **for** Halthurax.

**Sera:** Freed (returns her mote at peace) / Martyr (opens the path to the Source) / Defector (stands at his side).

## The Three Endings
Track which is earned.
- **Old Order Restored** — refuse + refill (healthy Ember + redeemed Court). Their dead stay dead; the world lives.
- **Pyrrhic Throne** — win with a gutted Ember; the world survives diminished.
- **Mercy of Never** — join him. No one dies again; the world stops being born. *Played straight, not punished.*

{{descriptive
The dagger — the one thing he can't answer: *"A world where no one dies is a world where no one is born."*
