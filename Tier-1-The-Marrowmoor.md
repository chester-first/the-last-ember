# TIER I — THE MARROWMOOR
### A fully runnable adventure for levels 1–4 (D&D 5e 2014)

This is the opening act of The Last Ember. It gives you four levels of play in a fog-bound borderland where the dead have begun walking home. Over the course of it, the party uncovers the Marrowmoor Vault, stops the Herald's anchoring rite, frees the revenant knight Sera Vant, and — for one un-fightable moment — meets the grieving god behind it all.

It runs in roughly 4–6 sessions and ends with the party at level 4 and Halthurax's first molt.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet (read this first)

Here is the shape of the adventure at a glance. The party starts in Lantern's Rest (the town), follows three leads out into the Moor, descends into the Marrowmoor Vault (the dungeon), fights the Herald and witnesses the Halthurax sighting, and the tier ends with his first molt.

Three things must happen along the way: the party bonds their items at the barrow-altar (R3), they free or fail Sera (R7), and they witness Halthurax alive (the R9 scene).

The Ember Clock starts at 12. Every time the party scatters an undead's mote — a quick kill — instead of returning it (which costs an action and a DC 10 check, or a turn spent), tick a tally. At session's end, if they scattered four or more, drop the Clock to 11. This teaches the central choice before it ever costs them.

For tone, aim for dread and warmth in the same breath. The undead here are pitiable — a baker walking home, a child at a door. This is horror that grieves, not horror that gloats.

### Level pacing
| Reach level | After… |
|---|---|
| 2 | Resolving the doorstep dead + one Moor lead |
| 3 | Entering the Vault and clearing the upper barrow |
| 4 | Defeating the Herald / surviving the Halthurax scene |

## 1. LANTERN'S REST (the town)

Lantern's Rest is a huddle of forty stone-and-thatch houses on a dry rise in the moor, ringed by old lanternposts kept burning day and night. Folk wisdom says flame keeps the dead from coming in. Lately the lanterns gutter for no wind.

Read aloud on arrival:
> "The fog out here doesn't lift — it just thins enough to lie about how far you can see.
> Lantern's Rest sits on the only dry ground for a league, every doorway hung with a burning
> lamp, every lamp leaning toward you like it wants to whisper. A woman on the green is nailing
> her own front door shut. She doesn't look up. 'He keeps comin' back,' she says. 'My Edon. He
> keeps comin' home.'"

### NPC roster — Lantern's Rest
| NPC | Role | Want | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mother Ostry** | Innkeep of the *Banked Coal*; town's spine | Keep her people alive | Gives the party the three Moor leads; calls in debts (Tie #2) |
| **Edon's widow, Halla** | Grieving wife | Her husband to *rest*, not return | The doorstep-dead encounter (§2) — the campaign's first mercy choice |
| **Warden Greel** | Last Emberwarden in the region, old and failing | Pass on his duty before he dies | The Emberwardens' first appearance; first Wardens-meter swings happen with him |
| **Coll the gravedigger** | Knows the moor's barrows | To be believed | Points the party to the Vault; comic relief that curdles |
| **The child at the Mourn-house** | A returned dead child, no malice | "To go home" | The adventure's gut-punch; how the party treats her sets the table's moral register |

Warden Greel matters most of all. He wears the gray. He explains the Ember, the return, and the fact that someone has stopped letting the dead go home. He's too weak to act himself. How the party treats Greel and his teaching is the first tick of Conflict #1. If they heed him and promise to return the dead, that's Wardens +1. If they mock him, scatter motes in front of him, or take Coll's "just burn 'em all" line, that's Wardens −1.

## 2. THE MOOR (three leads, level 1–2)

From town, three threads point toward the Vault. Run them in any order; two are enough to reach level 2–3.

### Lead A — The Doorstep Dead (the tutorial of mercy)
Edon keeps walking home each night. Halla can't bear to destroy him, and she also can't bear him. For the encounter, use the Zombie (MM) for Edon, but he does not attack — he only tries to reach his own door. He shoves, never strikes, unless he is attacked first. The "fight" is really a choice:
- **Scatter** him (kill the zombie): fast, brutal, and Halla screams. Tick the scatter tally.
- **Return** him: a PC spends an action and makes a **DC 10 Wisdom (Insight) or Religion** check, speaking him home. Edon sighs, his mote rises as a warm spark, and goes up. Halla weeps but thanks them. This is **Wardens +1.** Show the players that this is the kinder, slower way, and that it's always available.

This single scene is the whole campaign in miniature. Run it patiently.

### Lead B — The Mourn-house (the child)
This is an abandoned house on the moor's edge where returned dead gather at the windows, drawn to a small girl's lantern. Among them is the child, newly dead, who only wants to go home and doesn't understand she can't. For the optional combat, 3 **skeletons** and 2 **zombies** crowd the yard, but they're not hostile unless the party brings violence. A party that returns the child (DC 12, weighted toward roleplay) calms the whole yard, and the others follow her up. A party that scatters its way through ticks the tally hard and earns **Wardens −1.** Coll, if present, urges the cleansing-fire approach. This is the scene players will remember in a year.

### Lead C — Coll's map & the barrow-lights (the on-ramp to the dungeon)
Coll has seen "blue-fire lights" moving in the old Marrowmoor Vault, a barrow-complex out on the deep moor, and a robed figure going in and not coming out. There's one combat on the way, a level 1–2 encounter: a pack of **4 skeletons** rising from a sunken graveyard (CR 1/4 each). It's a classic first scrap, and you should let the party try returning versus scattering here too.

## 3. THE MARROWMOOR VAULT (the dungeon, levels 2–4)

This is an old Emberwarden barrow — once a holy place where the dead were returned — now hijacked by the Herald to anchor the Dimming in this region. It has two levels: the Upper Barrow (R1–R4) and the Ritual Deep (R5–R9).

### Map (schematic)

The Upper Barrow runs from R1 the Entry, to R2 the Cairn Hall, to R3 the Barrow-Altar (where the bonding happens), to R4 the Wight's Vigil. From the Cairn Hall, a stair leads down to the Deep. Down there, R6 the Gallery of the Returned opens onto R7 Sera's Cell and R8 the Hoard-Cells, and below them all lies R9 the Ritual Deep, which holds the Anchor and the Halthurax scene.

### Keyed rooms

**R1 — The Entry Mound.**
> "The barrow's mouth breathes cold. A single blue flame floats in the dark ahead, unattended,
> burning nothing."

The blue flame is a **will-o'-wisp** lure (CR 2) — a hoarded mote twisted into a guide-light. It leads the party deeper (toward R4's wight) if they follow blindly. The encounter is the wisp plus the choice to follow it or map carefully. There's also a trap: a collapsing cairn at the threshold (DC 13 Dex save, 2d6 bludgeoning, or spot it with a DC 12 Perception check).

**R2 — Cairn Hall.**
This is a long hall of stacked burial cairns. For the level 2 encounter, **6 skeletons** and **1 wight** (MM) rise from the central cairn when disturbed. The wight commands the skeletons; drop it and they collapse. Return-versus-scatter is available here, but the wight itself can only be scattered — its mote is too corrupted. Use this to foreshadow that some hoarding can't be undone gently.

**R3 — THE BARROW-ALTAR (the bonding happens here).**
> "The hall opens into a round chamber, and the cold goes warm. In the center, a low altar of
> black iron, and set into it a single coal that has been burning, somehow, for a thousand
> years. When you step close, it *leans* toward each of you — and the thing in your pack, the
> thing you've carried all this way, grows warm against your side."

This is the mechanical heart of session one. Each PC lays their chosen object on the coal; it drinks a mote of their fire and wakes (Appendix B — the *Kindled* tier comes online now). Roleplay each bonding individually, giving each PC a sentence of vision about what their item shows them. There's no combat here. This room is a gift. A desecration option does exist: the Herald wants this altar, and if the party later fails and he reaches it, he corrupts it. But on the first visit, it's theirs.

**R4 — The Wight's Vigil (mini-boss, level 2–3).**
This is a barrow-lord's tomb where the Herald has posted a **Wight** captain (MM, CR 3) with **2 specters** (MM) and **4 skeletons** as a rearguard over the stairs down. In combat, the specters phase through walls to harry casters, and the wight focuses whoever bonded the showiest item. As a reward, the wight carries an iron **Warden-key** (it opens R7, Sera's cell) and a **Cinder Vial** — a consumable that, as an action, returns all hoarded motes within 15 ft. It's usable once, and it's a real "save the room" button.

**R5 — Stair of the Deep.** This is a long descent. The walls are carved with the **Warden Creed**: "What is given, returns. What is hoarded, rots." A PC who reads it gets advantage on the Herald's later attempt to shake their conviction (R9). It's atmospheric, with no combat.

**R6 — The Gallery of the Returned.**
The walls hold murals of the old Emberwardens doing their sacred work — and newer, scratched-in additions in the Herald's hand that twist the images, showing the dead being kept instead of returned. A **Religion DC 13** check, or a Warden-blooded PC, reads the whole story here: the Herald serves a dragon who calls hoarding "mercy." This is the first explicit naming of the thesis. Optionally, a **shadow** (MM) for each PC peels off the murals if they linger and gloat or scatter — a soft punishment for cruelty, not a mandatory fight.

**R7 — Sera's Cell (the revenant ally — REQUIRED beat).**
Behind the iron Warden-door (opened with the R4 key) is a knight in shattered Warden-gray, chained to a hoard-stone, half here.
> "She's been dead a while — you can see that — but she's still *fighting* it. Her eyes find
> you and there's a person still in there, clawing to stay. 'Don't,' she rasps. 'Don't free me
> if you can't keep me *me*. I'd rather you scattered me than let him have what's left.'"

Sera Vant is a **revenant** (MM, CR 3), but she is not an enemy unless provoked. To free her, the party breaks the hoard-stone (AC 15, 30 HP, or a DC 15 check plus a returned mote), which releases her as a companion. Her **Tether starts at 7/10.** If the party scatters her or refuses her, the campaign loses its most important relationship thread — let that land, but make the merciful path clearly available.

As an NPC ally, Sera is loyal, grim, and soldierly. She remembers being an Emberwarden knight, and she carries a thread back to Halthurax in her chest that tightens every tier. She is the living face of Conflict #3. Use the revenant stats, but run her as a person, not a monster.

**R8 — The Hoard-Cells.**
This is a ring of cells holding hoarded motes as caged blue flames — the local hoard. For the level 3 encounter, **2 wights** and **8 skeletons** guard them, and there's a moral set-piece alongside the fight. The party can return the caged motes — each takes an action and a DC 12 check, and returning all 8 is a small **Wardens +2** that visibly brightens the room — or they can leave or scatter them to rush the Herald. This is the Ember Clock made tangible: they can see the souls they're choosing to save or spend.

**R9 — THE RITUAL DEEP (the Herald boss + the Halthurax scene).**

**Part A — The Herald (boss fight, level 3–4).**
The Herald stands at the anchor — a black-iron mirror of the R3 altar — mid-rite, with the local hoard feeding a column of gray fire toward the ceiling.
> "A figure in death-priest robes turns from the burning mirror, and where their face should be
> there's a sliver of golden scale, embedded like a relic. 'You came all this way,' they say,
> in two voices — theirs, and something vast underneath. 'Then let me show you what we're
> saving you from.'"

**THE HERALD — boss stat block (CR ~3, scale to party)**
- **Base:** Cult Fanatic (MM) with the following changes.
- **HP** 78 (was 33). **AC** 14. **Speed** 30.
- **Touch of the Hoard** (melee spell attack, +5, reach 5 ft): 3d6 necrotic, and the target's **hit point maximum is reduced** by the damage until a long rest. This is the signature campaign effect — introduce it here.
- **Spellcasting** (as Cult Fanatic): *command, inflict wounds, hold person, spiritual weapon.*
- **Gray Sermon (recharge 5–6):** the Herald preaches. Each enemy within 30 ft makes a **DC 13 Wisdom save** or is wracked with the grief of every soul it's lost — *frightened* and takes 2d8 psychic. A PC who read the Warden Creed (R5) has advantage.
- **Reinforce the Anchor (legendary-lite, 1/round, no action):** the Herald draws on the hoard to regain **10 HP** — unless the party has emptied the R8 Hoard-Cells. This is the mechanical payoff for mercy: parties who freed the souls upstream face a Herald who can't heal, while parties who rushed face a grind. Make this connection explicit in play.
- **Minions:** 2 **specters** + 4 **skeletons** rise from the anchor on initiative 20.

On the Herald's death, the anchor cracks, the gray column collapses, and the freed local hoard — every mote the party didn't already return — rushes upward in a roar of blue light. The Dimming lifts off the Marrowmoor. It's a real, visible victory. Then comes Part B.

**Part B — The Goldwyrm (a scene, NOT a fight).**
As the light clears, the broken mirror fills — and something looks through it.
> "The mirror doesn't show your reflection. It shows an eye, gold and ancient and big as a
> cathedral door, and behind it the curve of a dragon so vast your mind refuses the scale. He
> isn't angry. That's the worst part. He looks at you the way you'd look at a child who's just
> kicked over the only lamp in a blizzard.
>
> 'You think you've saved them,' Halthurax says, and his voice is *kind.* 'You've sent them back
> to be unmade and remade into strangers. I was *keeping* them. I kept your great-grandfathers
> safe and whole for a thousand years while your Wardens fed them to the fire and called it
> mercy.' The eye narrows, almost gentle. 'Go home, little embers. Grow. I can wait. And when
> you're bigger, come and see what I'm building. Then tell me death is worth keeping.'
>
> The mirror goes dark. The barrow is just a barrow again. But the cold has a shape now, and it
> knows your faces."

Run this as awe, not as a threat encounter. Halthurax is CR ~17 (Ancient Gold Dragon) and will not fight. If players attack the mirror, it shatters harmlessly and he simply withdraws — which is more unsettling than retaliation. He plants the campaign's question — is death worth keeping? — and leaves. This is the molt: he no longer needs the Material Plane and withdraws fully into the planes, beginning his change into the Dragon-Wraith.

## 4. AFTERMATH & HOOKS TO TIER II

- **The party hits level 4.** The first legacy-item milestone (Burning) fires at the start of the Tier II arc — tell players their items are about to grow.
- **The Marrowmoor brightens.** The lanterns hold their flame again, Halla sleeps, and the child rests. Let the players feel they won this region. They did.
- **Sera's pull.** If she was freed, Sera feels something tugging her "toward the deep woods, toward a place where the light is too bright to be the world." That's the fey crossing to the Verdant Pyre — the gateway to Tier II.
- **Warden Greel dies** (or, if they earned it, names the party Wardens-in-training). Either way, the gray duty passes to the PCs, and the Wardens meter they built with Greel is locked as its Tier I baseline.
- **The clocks at end of Tier I:** record the Ember Clock (12 or 11), the Wardens meter, and Sera's Tether (7, adjusted by party choices). Carry them into the Feywild.
