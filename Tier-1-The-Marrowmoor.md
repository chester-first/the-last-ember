# TIER I — THE MARROWMOOR
### A fully runnable adventure for levels 1–4 (D&D 5e 2014)

The opening act of **The Last Ember**. Four levels of play in a fog-bound borderland where
the dead have begun walking home. The party uncovers the **Marrowmoor Vault**, stops the
**Herald's** anchoring rite, frees the revenant knight **Sera Vant**, and — for one
un-fightable moment — meets the grieving god behind it all.

**Runs in ~4–6 sessions.** Ends with the party at level 4 and Halthurax's first molt.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet (read this first)

- **Where it goes:** Lantern's Rest (town) → the Moor (3 leads) → the Marrowmoor Vault
  (dungeon) → the Herald (boss) + Halthurax sighting (scene) → first molt.
- **Three things that MUST happen:** (1) the party bonds their items at the **barrow-altar**
  (R3); (2) they free or fail **Sera** (R7); (3) they witness **Halthurax** alive (R9 scene).
- **The Ember Clock starts at 12.** Every time the party **scatters** an undead's mote
  (quick kill) instead of **returning** it (an action + a DC 10 check or a turn spent),
  tick a tally — at session's end, if they scattered 4+, drop the Clock to 11. This teaches
  the central choice before it ever costs them.
- **Tone:** dread and warmth in the same breath. The undead here are *pitiable* — a baker
  walking home, a child at a door. This is horror that grieves, not horror that gloats.

### Level pacing
| Reach level | After… |
|---|---|
| 2 | Resolving the doorstep dead + one Moor lead |
| 3 | Entering the Vault and clearing the upper barrow |
| 4 | Defeating the Herald / surviving the Halthurax scene |

## 1. LANTERN'S REST (the town)

A huddle of forty stone-and-thatch houses on a dry rise in the moor, ringed by old
**lanternposts** kept burning day and night — folk wisdom says flame keeps the dead from
coming in. Lately the lanterns gutter for no wind.

**Read aloud on arrival:**
> "The fog out here doesn't lift — it just thins enough to lie about how far you can see.
> Lantern's Rest sits on the only dry ground for a league, every doorway hung with a burning
> lamp, every lamp leaning toward you like it wants to whisper. A woman on the green is nailing
> her own front door shut. She doesn't look up. 'He keeps comin' back,' she says. 'My Edon. He
> keeps comin' home.'"

### NPC roster — Lantern's Rest
| NPC | Role | Want | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Mother Ostry** | Innkeep of the *Banked Coal*; town's spine | Keep her people alive | Gives the party the three Moor leads; calls in debts (Tie #2) |
| **Edon's widow, Halla** | Grieving wife | Her husband to *rest*, not return | The doorstep-dead encounter (§2) — the campaign's first mercy choice |
| **Warden Greel** | Last Emberwarden in the region, old and failing | Pass on his duty before he dies | The Emberwardens' first appearance; **first Wardens-meter swings happen with him** |
| **Coll the gravedigger** | Knows the moor's barrows | To be believed | Points the party to the Vault; comic relief that curdles |
| **The child at the Mourn-house** | A returned dead child, no malice | "To go home" | The adventure's gut-punch; how the party treats her sets the table's moral register |

**Warden Greel matters most.** He wears the gray. He explains the Ember, the return, and that
"someone's stopped letting them go home." He's too weak to act. **How the party treats Greel
and his teaching is the first tick of Conflict #1:** heed him and promise to return the dead →
Wardens +1; mock him, scatter motes in front of him, or take Coll's "just burn 'em all" line →
Wardens −1.

## 2. THE MOOR (three leads, level 1–2)

From town, three threads point at the Vault. Run them in any order; two are enough to reach
level 2–3.

### Lead A — The Doorstep Dead (the tutorial of mercy)
**Edon** keeps walking home each night. Halla can't bear to destroy him; she also can't bear
him. **Encounter:** Edon (use **Zombie**, MM, but he does not attack — he only tries to reach
his own door; he shoves, never strikes, unless attacked). The "fight" is a *choice*:
- **Scatter** him (kill the zombie): fast, brutal, Halla screams. Tick the scatter tally.
- **Return** him: a PC spends an action and makes a **DC 10 Wisdom (Insight) or Religion**
  check, speaking him home — Edon sighs, his mote rises as a warm spark, and *goes up*. Halla
  weeps but thanks them. **Wardens +1.** Show the players: *this is the kinder, slower way, and
  it's always available.*

This single scene is the whole campaign in miniature. Run it patiently.

### Lead B — The Mourn-house (the child)
An abandoned house on the moor's edge where returned dead gather at the windows, drawn to a
small girl's lantern. Among them: **the child**, newly dead, who only wants to go home and
doesn't understand she can't. **Encounter (optional combat):** 3 **skeletons** and 2
**zombies** crowd the yard; they're not hostile unless the party brings violence. A party that
*returns* the child (DC 12, roleplay-weighted) calms the whole yard — the others follow her up.
A party that scatters its way through ticks the tally hard and earns **Wardens −1.** Coll, if
present, urges the cleansing-fire approach. **This is the scene players will remember in a year.**

### Lead C — Coll's map & the barrow-lights (the on-ramp to the dungeon)
Coll has seen "blue-fire lights" moving in the old **Marrowmoor Vault**, a barrow-complex out
on the deep moor, and a robed figure going in and not coming out. Combat on the way: **1
encounter, level 1–2** — a pack of **4 skeletons** rising from a sunken graveyard (CR 1/4 each;
classic first scrap; let the party try returning vs. scattering here too).

## 3. THE MARROWMOOR VAULT (the dungeon, levels 2–4)

An old Emberwarden barrow — once a holy place where the dead were *returned* — now hijacked by
the Herald to **anchor the Dimming** in this region. Two levels: the **Upper Barrow** (R1–R4)
and the **Ritual Deep** (R5–R9).

### Map (schematic)
```
                          [R9 RITUAL DEEP — the Anchor & Halthurax scene]
                                       |
                          [R8 The Hoard-Cells]---[R7 Sera's Cell]
                                       |
   UPPER BARROW          [R6 The Gallery of the Returned]
   --------------                      |
   [R1 Entry] -- [R2 Cairn Hall] -- [R3 Barrow-Altar (BONDING)] -- [R4 Wight's Vigil]
                                       |
                                  (stair down to the Deep)
```

### Keyed rooms

**R1 — The Entry Mound.**
> "The barrow's mouth breathes cold. A single blue flame floats in the dark ahead, unattended,
> burning nothing."
The blue flame is a **will-o'-wisp** lure (CR 2) — a hoarded mote twisted into a guide-light.
It leads the party *deeper* (toward R4's wight) if they follow blindly. **Encounter:** the
wisp + the choice to follow or map carefully. Trap: a **collapsing cairn** at the threshold
(DC 13 Dex save, 2d6 bludgeoning, or spot it DC 12 Perception).

**R2 — Cairn Hall.**
A long hall of stacked burial cairns. **Encounter (level 2):** **6 skeletons** + **1 wight**
(MM) that rises from the central cairn when disturbed. The wight commands the skeletons; drop
it and they collapse. *Return-vs-scatter* is available but the wight itself can only be
scattered (its mote is too corrupted — foreshadow that some hoarding can't be undone gently).

**R3 — THE BARROW-ALTAR (the bonding happens here).**
> "The hall opens into a round chamber, and the cold goes warm. In the center, a low altar of
> black iron, and set into it a single coal that has been burning, somehow, for a thousand
> years. When you step close, it *leans* toward each of you — and the thing in your pack, the
> thing you've carried all this way, grows warm against your side."
This is the mechanical heart of session one. Each PC lays their chosen object on the coal; it
**drinks a mote of their fire and wakes** (Appendix B, *Kindled* tier comes online now). Roleplay
each bonding individually — a sentence of vision per PC about what their item *shows* them.
**No combat here. This room is a gift.** (A desecration option exists: the Herald wants this
altar; if the party later fails and the Herald reaches it, he corrupts it — but on first visit,
it's theirs.)

**R4 — The Wight's Vigil (mini-boss, level 2–3).**
A barrow-lord's tomb where the Herald has posted a **Wight** captain (MM, CR 3) with **2
specters** (MM) and **4 skeletons** as a rearguard over the stairs down. Tactics: specters
phase through walls to harry casters; the wight focuses whoever bonded the showiest item.
**Reward:** the wight carries an **iron Warden-key** (opens R7, Sera's cell) and a **Cinder
Vial** (consumable: as an action, return *all* hoarded motes within 15 ft — usable once; a
real "save the room" button).

**R5 — Stair of the Deep.** A long descent; the walls are carved with the **Warden Creed**
("*What is given, returns. What is hoarded, rots.*"). A PC who reads it gets advantage on the
Herald's later attempt to *shake their conviction* (R9). Atmospheric; no combat.

**R6 — The Gallery of the Returned.**
Murals of the old Emberwardens doing their sacred work — and newer, scratched-in additions in
the Herald's hand twisting the images: the dead being *kept* instead of returned. A
**Religion DC 13** or a Warden-blooded PC reads the whole story here: *the Herald serves a
dragon who calls hoarding "mercy."* First explicit naming of the thesis. **Optional:** a
**shadow** (MM) for each PC peels off the murals if they linger and gloat or scatter — a soft
punishment for cruelty, not a mandatory fight.

**R7 — Sera's Cell (the revenant ally — REQUIRED beat).**
Behind the iron Warden-door (R4 key), a knight in shattered Warden-gray, chained to a hoard-
stone, *half here.*
> "She's been dead a while — you can see that — but she's still *fighting* it. Her eyes find
> you and there's a person still in there, clawing to stay. 'Don't,' she rasps. 'Don't free me
> if you can't keep me *me*. I'd rather you scattered me than let him have what's left.'"
**Sera Vant**, a **revenant** (MM, CR 3) — but **not an enemy unless provoked.** Freeing her:
breaking the hoard-stone (AC 15, 30 HP, or a DC 15 check + a returned mote) releases her *as a
companion.* Her **Tether starts at 7/10.** If the party scatters her or refuses her, the
campaign loses its most important relationship thread — let that land, but make the merciful
path clearly available.
**Sera as an NPC ally:** loyal, grim, soldierly; remembers being an Emberwarden knight; carries
a thread back to Halthurax in her chest that *tightens every tier.* She is the living face of
Conflict #3. Use revenant stats but run her as a person, not a monster.

**R8 — The Hoard-Cells.**
A ring of cells holding hoarded motes as caged blue flames — the local hoard. **Encounter
(level 3):** **2 wights** + **8 skeletons** guarding them, plus the moral set-piece: the party
can **return the caged motes** (each takes an action + DC 12; returning all 8 is a small
*Wardens +2* and visibly brightens the room) or **leave/scatter** them to rush the Herald.
**This is the Ember Clock made tangible** — they can *see* the souls they're choosing to save
or spend.

**R9 — THE RITUAL DEEP (the Herald boss + the Halthurax scene).**

**Part A — The Herald (boss fight, level 3–4).**
The Herald stands at the anchor — a black-iron mirror of the R3 altar — mid-rite, the local
hoard feeding a column of gray fire toward the ceiling.
> "A figure in death-priest robes turns from the burning mirror, and where their face should be
> there's a sliver of golden scale, embedded like a relic. 'You came all this way,' they say,
> in two voices — theirs, and something vast underneath. 'Then let me show you what we're
> saving you from.'"

**THE HERALD — boss stat block (CR ~3, scale to party)**
- **Base:** Cult Fanatic (MM) with the following changes.
- **HP** 78 (was 33). **AC** 14. **Speed** 30.
- **Touch of the Hoard** (melee spell attack, +5, reach 5 ft): 3d6 necrotic, and the target's
  **hit point maximum is reduced** by the damage until a long rest (the signature campaign
  effect — introduce it here).
- **Spellcasting** (as Cult Fanatic): *command, inflict wounds, hold person, spiritual weapon.*
- **Gray Sermon (recharge 5–6):** the Herald *preaches.* Each enemy within 30 ft makes a **DC
  13 Wisdom save** or is wracked with the grief of every soul it's lost — *frightened* and
  takes 2d8 psychic. A PC who read the Warden Creed (R5) has advantage.
- **Reinforce the Anchor (legendary-lite, 1/round, no action):** the Herald draws on the hoard
  to regain **10 HP** — *unless* the party has emptied the R8 Hoard-Cells. **Mechanical payoff
  for mercy:** parties who freed the souls upstream face a Herald who can't heal; parties who
  rushed face a grind. Make this connection explicit in play.
- **Minions:** 2 **specters** + 4 **skeletons** rise from the anchor on initiative 20.

**On the Herald's death:** the anchor cracks, the gray column collapses, and the freed local
hoard — every mote the party didn't already return — rushes upward in a roar of blue light.
The Dimming *lifts* off the Marrowmoor. Real, visible victory. **Then Part B.**

**Part B — The Goldwyrm (a scene, NOT a fight).**
As the light clears, the broken mirror *fills* — and something looks through it.
> "The mirror doesn't show your reflection. It shows an eye, gold and ancient and big as a
> cathedral door, and behind it the curve of a dragon so vast your mind refuses the scale. He
> isn't angry. That's the worst part. He looks at you the way you'd look at a child who's just
> kicked over the only lamp in a blizzard.
>
> 'You think you've saved them,' Halthurax says, and his voice is *kind.* 'You've sent them back
> to be unmade and remade into strangers. I was *keeping* them. I kept your great-grandfathers
> safe and whole for a thousand years while your Wardens fed them to the fire and called it
> mercy.' The eye narrows, almost gentle. 'Go home, little embers. Grow. I can wait. And when
> you're bigger, come and see what I'm building. Then tell me death is worth keeping.'
>
> The mirror goes dark. The barrow is just a barrow again. But the cold has a shape now, and it
> knows your faces."

**Run this as awe, not a threat encounter.** Halthurax is CR ~17 (Ancient Gold Dragon) and will
not fight. If players attack the mirror, it shatters harmlessly and he simply withdraws — *more*
unsettling than retaliation. He plants the campaign's question — *is death worth keeping?* — and
leaves. This is the molt: he no longer needs the Material Plane and withdraws fully into the
planes, beginning his change to the Dragon-Wraith.

## 4. AFTERMATH & HOOKS TO TIER II

- **The party hits level 4.** First **legacy-item milestone (Burning)** fires at the start of
  the Tier II arc — tell players their items are about to grow.
- **The Marrowmoor brightens.** Lanterns hold their flame again; Halla sleeps; the child rests.
  Let the players feel they *won this region.* They did.
- **Sera's pull.** If freed, Sera feels something tugging her "toward the deep woods, toward a
  place where the light is too bright to be the world." That's the **fey crossing** to the
  Verdant Pyre — the gateway to Tier II.
- **Warden Greel dies** (or names the party Wardens-in-training, if they earned it). Either way,
  the gray duty passes to the PCs — and the **Wardens meter** they built with Greel is locked
  as its Tier I baseline.
- **The clocks at end of Tier I:** record the Ember Clock (12 or 11), the Wardens meter, and
  Sera's Tether (7, ±party choices). Carry them into the Feywild.
