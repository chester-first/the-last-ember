# TIER III — THE HOARD-KINGDOM
### A fully runnable arc for levels 11–16 (D&D 5e 2014) — the Shadowfell

The party descends the soul-fall into the Shadowfell, where Halthurax — now a true Dracolich — has built a kingdom out of hoarded souls: the gray city of Ossuara. This is the horror tier, and the most morally loaded of all, because Ossuara is not a torment-pit. It is pleasant. Its citizens are happy hoarded dead who do not want to be freed. Player death is expected here, so run it that way.

This arc runs about seven to ten sessions. It ends at level 16, with the kingdom shattered, the great hoard freed, and Halthurax molting toward the Source.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet

Here is the shape of the tier. The party falls through the soul-fall, passes the Gray Gates, and arrives at Ossuara, the city hub. From there they meet the Grateful Dead, navigate the Death-Court (the politics, plus Sera's reckoning), fight the Siege of the Pale Hoard, and finally reach the Throne-Vault for the dracolich boss.

Three beats have to happen. First, the party has to confront that Ossuara's dead don't want saving. Second, Sera's fate vector locks when she meets her old liege, the Chancellor. Third comes the dracolich fight, where a PC death is expected and the Ember Clock crisis turns on.

A couple of things to keep in mind. Legacy items hit BLAZING at the start of this tier. And the Ember Clock reaches crisis: it drops to somewhere between 4 and 2 this tier, which means resurrection now requires another's mote, or it returns the PC Touched. The death subsystem in `The-Last-Ember.md` covers the details.

## 1. OSSUARA (the city hub)

Down the soul-fall, the Shadowfell opens onto a city that should be a graveyard and is instead a capital — lamplit boulevards, gray gardens, a populace of the comfortable dead who tip their hats. They are not suffering. That is the horror.

Read aloud:
> "You brace for a charnel pit. You get a city at dusk — clean streets, lit windows, a square where pale folk trade gossip and gray bread. A woman waves at you like you're expected. 'New arrivals?' she says, kindly. 'It's frightening at first, I know. But no one dies here. No one *leaves* here. He keeps us all.' She says it like a blessing. She believes it is one."

Ossuara is built on a seductive truth: Halthurax really has made a place where the dead are safe, remembered, and whole. The party came here to free these souls. The trouble is that the souls would rather stay.

## 2. THE GRATEFUL DEAD (levels 11–12)

This is exploration and moral horror through Ossuara. The party meets souls they thought they'd saved — villagers from Lantern's Rest, the child from the Mourn-house, fey from the Pyre — who were swept here when their motes were "freed" into a Source too drained to rekindle them. And they blame the party.

> "You know her. The child from the Mourn-house — you sent her home, you *returned* her, you felt good about it. She's here now. She's still a child. She remembers. 'You said I could go home,' she says. 'There was no home. There was just the cold, and then *he* found me, and he was the only one who came.'"

This is the campaign auditing its own central choice. Was returning souls mercy, or did the party feed a failing Source? The honest answer depends on the Ember Clock. A party that kept the Clock healthy finds that most returned souls did rekindle and aren't here; a party that let it drain finds Ossuara full of their good intentions. Make the Clock's number mean exactly this. No combat is required — this is a gut-check the players carry into every later choice.

## 3. THE DEATH-COURT (levels 13–14)

This is politics among the dracolich's lieutenants in the Pallid Court. The party can move through it by guile, force, or alliance. Two threads matter most.

### 3.1 Sera's Reckoning (Conflict #3 LOCKS here)
The Chancellor is Lord Vant — the liege who sent Sera to die in the Marrowmoor Vault and let her be hoarded. Their confrontation is the tier's emotional spine, and how it goes sets Sera's endgame, combined with her Tether value.

- **High Tether (7+) and the party backs her:** Sera forgives or condemns on her own terms, reclaims her name, and walks out whole — set on the path to be freed at the finale.
- **Mid Tether (3–6):** the confrontation costs her; she spends self to win it and is set toward a martyr's end.
- **Low Tether (0–2):** Vant reclaims her — reminds her she was always his, and Halthurax's pull finishes the job. She is set to defect. Devastating, and earned.

You can play this as a tense social scene, a duel, or both. No outcome requires the party to win a fight — it's about the relationship they built over three tiers.

### 3.2 Kalaurith's Bargain (a dragon ally)
The undead red dragon Kalaurith (run her as an Adult Red Dragon plus dracolich-lite, around CR 15) hates serving Halthurax. The party can recruit her for the Siege or the Throne — at a price. She wants the throne-vault's hoard for herself, or her freedom, or a PC's legacy item. So she is a dragon ally and a dragon temptation at once, and a second voice in the campaign's argument among dragons about death.

## 4. THE SIEGE OF THE PALE HOARD (level 15)

This is a full undead-army set-piece. The party assaults the Pale Hoard, the vault where Halthurax keeps the densest concentration of his distributed phylactery. General Ossix and the Pale Legion defend it.

Run it as a layered battle with three fronts the party divides between, tracked with a simple clock.

1. **The Wall** — break the bone-rampart in a siege against 2 wights, a bone golem (use a Stone Golem reskinned, CR 10), and ranged skeleton archers.
2. **The Gate** — General Ossix (use a Death Knight, MM, CR 17, scaled to the party) holds it; a worthy duel.
3. **The Vault Core** — three Phylactery-Pyres (AC 17, 60 HP each) that must be destroyed to weaken the dracolich in the finale. Destroying them here is what lets the party actually hurt Halthurax next session, so tie the Siege's success directly to the boss's difficulty.

A redeemed Court, a recruited Kalaurith, or freed allies show up here as reinforcements. A corrupted party fights short-handed.

---

## 5. THE THRONE-VAULT — tier boss (level 16)

Halthurax the Dracolich sits on his throne of fused souls. This is the first time he can be truly hurt, and the first fight where a PC death is expected. Run it lethal.

The full stat block for the dracolich is in `Appendix-A-Halthurax.md` (Form III, around CR 21). Here are the key beats.

- **Distributed Phylactery regen:** he regains 30 HP each turn unless the Soul-Pyres are down. The ones the party destroyed in the Siege (§4) are already gone, which rewards the prior session. Any remaining pyres must be killed mid-fight (AC 17, 60 HP).
- **Hoardfire breath, lair actions, and Call the Grateful Dead:** the lair actions raise the dead, send grasping hands, and slam down a bone portcullis. Call the Grateful Dead lets a hoarded citizen bodyblock an attack — turning the city's "innocents" into his shield, the tier's thesis weaponized.
- **The expected death:** the Ember Clock is low, so a PC who falls here may not come back easily, or comes back Touched. This is by design, and it makes the climb to the Source personal.

When he hits 0 HP with the pyres down, the kingdom shatters. The great mass of the hoard tears free in the single biggest Ember event of the campaign. But the Source is too weak to catch it all, and the loose souls stream upward after Halthurax, who rides them to the one warm place left. His final molt: he goes to become the Source. The party must follow.

---

## 6. AFTERMATH & HOOKS TO TIER IV

The party hits level 16. Carry forward the Ember Clock (now in crisis), the Court allegiance, Sera's locked fate-vector, and any dragon ally. Legacy items reach RADIANT at the start of Tier IV.

There is also the matter of the fallen. Any PC permanently lost in the crisis is not necessarily gone forever — the Source is the one place the Dimming can be undone. Their restoration becomes a personal stake in the final tier.

Finally, the way up. The soul-stream now runs upward, against the current, to the Emberthrone. The whole campaign has been a descent, from the Material plane to the Feywild to the Shadowfell; the finale is the climb back toward the light.
