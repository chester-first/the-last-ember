# TIER III — THE HOARD-KINGDOM
### A fully runnable arc for levels 11–16 (D&D 5e 2014) — the Shadowfell

The party descends the soul-fall into the **Shadowfell**, where Halthurax — now a true
**Dracolich** — has built a *kingdom* out of hoarded souls: the gray city of **Ossuara**. This
is the horror tier and the most morally loaded, because Ossuara is not a torment-pit. It is
*pleasant.* Its citizens are happy hoarded dead who do not want to be freed. **Player death is
expected here** — run it that way.

**Runs ~7–10 sessions.** Ends at level 16, the kingdom shattered, the great hoard freed, and
Halthurax's molt toward the Source.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet

- **Where it goes:** the Soul-Fall → the Gray Gates → Ossuara (city hub) → the Grateful Dead →
  the Death-Court (politics + Sera's reckoning) → the Siege of the Pale Hoard → the Throne-Vault
  (dracolich boss).
- **Three must-happen beats:** (1) the party confronts that Ossuara's dead **don't want
  saving**; (2) **Sera's fate vector locks** when she meets her old liege, the Chancellor; (3)
  the **dracolich fight**, where a PC death is *expected* and the Ember Clock crisis turns on.
- **Legacy items hit BLAZING** at the start of this tier.
- **Ember Clock crisis:** the Clock hits 4–2 this tier — resurrection now requires another's
  mote or returns the PC **Touched** (see the death subsystem in `The-Last-Ember.md`).

### Level pacing
| Reach level | After… |

## 1. OSSUARA (the city hub)

Down the soul-fall, the Shadowfell opens onto a city that should be a graveyard and is instead
a *capital* — lamplit boulevards, gray gardens, a populace of the comfortable dead who tip
their hats. They are not suffering. That is the horror.

**Read aloud:**
> "You brace for a charnel pit. You get a city at dusk — clean streets, lit windows, a square
> where pale folk trade gossip and gray bread. A woman waves at you like you're expected. 'New
> arrivals?' she says, kindly. 'It's frightening at first, I know. But no one dies here. No one
> *leaves* here. He keeps us all.' She says it like a blessing. She believes it is one."

Ossuara is built on a seductive truth: Halthurax really has made a place where the dead are
safe, remembered, and whole. The party came to *free* these souls. The souls would rather stay.

## 2. THE GRATEFUL DEAD (levels 11–12)

Exploration and moral horror through Ossuara. The party meets souls they *thought* they'd saved
— villagers from Lantern's Rest, the child from the Mourn-house, fey from the Pyre — who were
swept here when their motes were "freed" into a Source too drained to rekindle them. **They blame
the party.**

> "You know her. The child from the Mourn-house — you sent her home, you *returned* her, you
> felt good about it. She's here now. She's still a child. She remembers. 'You said I could go
> home,' she says. 'There was no home. There was just the cold, and then *he* found me, and he
> was the only one who came.'"

**This is the campaign auditing its own central choice.** Was returning souls mercy, or did the
party feed a failing Source? The honest answer depends on the **Ember Clock**: a party that kept
the Clock healthy finds most returned souls *did* rekindle and aren't here; a party that let it
drain finds Ossuara full of their good intentions. **Make the Clock's number mean this.** No
combat required — this is a gut-check the players carry into every later choice.

## 3. THE DEATH-COURT (levels 13–14)

Politics among the dracolich's lieutenants in the Pallid Court. The party can move through it by
guile, force, or alliance. Key threads:

### 3.1 Sera's Reckoning (Conflict #3 LOCKS here)
The Chancellor is **Lord Vant** — the liege who sent Sera to die in the Marrowmoor Vault and let
her be hoarded. Their confrontation is the tier's emotional spine, and **how it goes sets Sera's
endgame** (combined with her Tether value):
- **High Tether (7+) + the party backs her:** Sera forgives or condemns *on her own terms*,
  reclaims her name, and walks out whole — set on the path to be **freed** at the finale.
- **Mid Tether (3–6):** the confrontation costs her; she spends self to win it and is set toward
  a **martyr's** end.
- **Low Tether (0–2):** Vant *reclaims* her — reminds her she was always his, and Halthurax's pull
  finishes the job. She is set to **defect.** Devastating, and earned.
This can be played as a tense social scene, a duel, or both. No outcome requires the party to
"win a fight" — it's about the relationship they built over three tiers.

### 3.2 Kalaurith's Bargain (a dragon ally)
The undead red dragon **Kalaurith** (Appendix-style: **Adult Red Dragon** + dracolich-lite,
CR ~15) hates serving Halthurax. The party can recruit her for the Siege/Throne — at a price
(she wants the throne-vault's hoard for herself, or her freedom, or a PC's legacy item). A
**dragon ally vs. a dragon temptation** — and a second voice in the campaign's argument among
dragons about death.

## 4. THE SIEGE OF THE PALE HOARD (level 15)

A full undead-army set-piece: the party assaults the **Pale Hoard**, the vault where Halthurax
keeps the densest concentration of his distributed phylactery. **General Ossix** and the **Pale
Legion** defend it.

**Run as a layered battle (3 fronts the party divides between — use a simple clock):**
1. **The Wall** — break the bone-rampart (siege; **2 wights**, **bone golem** [use **Stone
   Golem** reskinned, CR 10], ranged **skeleton archers**).
2. **The Gate** — **General Ossix** (use **Death Knight**, MM, CR 17, scaled to party) holds it;
   a worthy duel.
3. **The Vault Core** — **3 Phylactery-Pyres** (AC 17, 60 HP each) that must be destroyed to
   weaken the dracolich in the finale — *destroying them here is what lets the party actually hurt
   Halthurax next session.* Tie the Siege's success directly to the boss's difficulty.

A redeemed Court / recruited Kalaurith / freed allies show up here as reinforcements. A corrupted
party fights short-handed.

---

## 5. THE THRONE-VAULT — tier boss (level 16)

Halthurax the **Dracolich** on his throne of fused souls. **This is the first time he can be
truly hurt — and the first fight where a PC death is expected.** Run it lethal.

**THE DRACOLICH** — full block in `Appendix-A-Halthurax.md` (Form III, CR ~21). Key beats:
- **Distributed Phylactery regen:** he regains 30 HP each turn *unless* the **Soul-Pyres** are
  down — the ones the party destroyed in the Siege (§4) are already gone, rewarding the prior
  session. Any remaining must be killed mid-fight (AC 17, 60 HP).
- **Hoardfire breath** + **lair actions** (raise the dead, grasping hands, bone portcullis) +
  **Call the Grateful Dead** (a hoarded citizen bodyblocks an attack — turning the city's
  "innocents" into his shield, the tier's thesis weaponized).
- **The expected death:** the Ember Clock is low; a PC who falls here may not come back easily —
  or comes back **Touched.** This is by design and it makes the climb to the Source personal.

**On 0 HP (with pyres down):** the kingdom shatters. The great mass of the hoard tears free — the
single biggest Ember event of the campaign. But the Source is too weak to catch it all, and the
loose souls stream upward after Halthurax, who rides them to the one warm place left. **Final
molt: he goes to *become* the Source.** The party must follow.

---

## 6. AFTERMATH & HOOKS TO TIER IV

- **Party hits level 16.** Carry forward the Ember Clock (now in crisis), the Court allegiance,
  **Sera's locked fate-vector**, and any dragon ally. **Legacy items reach RADIANT** at the start
  of Tier IV.
- **The fallen.** Any PC permanently lost in the crisis is *not necessarily gone forever* — the
  Source is the one place the Dimming can be undone. Their restoration becomes a personal stake in
  the final tier.
- **The way up.** The soul-stream now runs *upward*, against the current, to the **Emberthrone**.
  The whole campaign has been a descent (Material → Feywild → Shadowfell); the finale is the
  climb back toward the light.
