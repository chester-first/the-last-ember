# APPENDIX A — Halthurax, the Goldwyrm Who Would Not Burn
### The recurring villain's four-form stat progression

Halthurax is built to be **fought once per tier**, each time as a more-complete being, and
to be *truly* killable only in Tier IV. These blocks are written as **deltas off existing
5e 2014 monsters** so you can run them at the table without inventing a whole stat block —
take the base creature, apply the changes, drop in the **Hoard Phylactery** rule.

> **The Hoard Phylactery (universal rule, all forms).** Halthurax's phylactery is the mass
> of embers he has hoarded across the planes. While even one tier's hoard remains unfreed,
> if his current form drops to 0 HP it is **destroyed, not killed** — he reconstitutes in the
> next plane after the tier ends. He is only permanently slain in Tier IV, and only when the
> party has reduced his "hoard pool" (see Form IV) to 0.

---

## FORM 0 — The Living Wyrm (Tier I sighting only, ~CR 17)
**Base:** Adult/Ancient **Gold Dragon** (use Ancient Gold Dragon, MM). Run as written.
**Role:** This is **not a fight.** He appears for one scene at the bottom of the Marrowmoor
Vault, vast and golden and *sad.* He speaks, warns the party off for their own good, and
leaves. If players attack, he does not kill them — he restrains, lectures, and departs, which
is more unsettling than a slaughter. Establishes: *the monster was the protector, and he is
not cruel.*

**The Herald (Tier I actual boss, CR ~3):** Halthurax's mortal agent. **Base:** Cult Fanatic
(MM) with these changes: HP 65; gains *Touch of the Hoard* (melee spell attack, +5, 3d6
necrotic, target's hit point maximum reduced by the damage until a long rest); commands 2d4
skeletons/zombies and one **wight**. Killing the Herald frees the Marrowmoor hoard and
triggers Halthurax's first molt.

---

## FORM I → FORM II — The Dragon-Wraith (Tier II boss, CR ~13)
**Base:** Adult **Gold Dragon** (MM), then apply the **wraith-dragon template**:
- **Type** becomes Undead. Gains **Resistance** to necrotic, acid, cold; **Vulnerability**
  none; **Immunity** to poison, exhaustion, frightened, charmed.
- **Incorporeal Movement** (as Wraith): move through creatures/objects, 1d10 force if it ends
  there.
- Replace the fire breath with **Graying Breath** (Recharge 5–6): 60-ft cone, DC 17 Con,
  10d8 necrotic on a fail (half on save), and each creature that fails has its **hit point
  maximum reduced** by the amount until a long rest. Plants and fey light in the area wither.
- **Soul Siphon (1/turn, free):** when it reduces a creature to 0 HP, Halthurax gains temp
  HP equal to half the creature's level/CR ×5, and that creature's mote is **hoarded** unless
  freed.
- **Legendary Resistance (2/day)** and **3 legendary actions** (Detect / Tail Attack / Wing
  of Ash, the last imposing a DC 16 Str save or knocked prone in gray fog).

**Tier-II behavior:** First, a **hunt** set-piece at lvl 9 (he stalks, harries, retreats —
can't be killed yet, it's not time). Then the real fight at the Pyre's Heart, lvl 10. On 0 HP:
form destroyed, hoard partly freed, he molts toward the Shadowfell.

---

## FORM II → FORM III — Halthurax the Dracolich (Tier III boss, CR ~21)
**Base:** **Adult Gold Dragon** statline scaled up + the **Dracolich** template (see MToF /
classic dracolich rules), or build off **Ancient Gold Dragon** with these changes:
- **Type** Undead. **Immunities:** poison, necrotic; **conditions:** charmed, frightened,
  paralyzed, poisoned, exhaustion. **Resistances:** cold, lightning; **bludg./pierc./slash.
  from nonmagical attacks.**
- **Legendary Resistance (3/day).**
- **Phylactery, distributed:** while in his Hoard-Kingdom, at the start of each of his turns
  he regains **30 HP** unless his throne-vault's **Soul-Pyres** (3 of them, AC 17, 60 HP each,
  scattered in the room) have all been destroyed. Tactically, the party must *split focus*
  between the dragon and the pyres — this is the first time their choices can actually stop
  his regeneration.
- **Breath — Hoardfire (Recharge 5–6):** 90-ft cone, DC 22 Dex, 16d6 necrotic + fire, half on
  save; a creature reduced to 0 by it is **claimed** (death save failures count double until
  freed).
- **Lair actions** in Ossuara: raise 2d6 skeletons from the floor; close a portcullis of bone;
  fill a 20-ft cube with grasping hands (difficult terrain + DC 18 Str or restrained).
- **3 legendary actions** as Ancient Gold + **Call the Grateful Dead** (2 actions): a hoarded
  citizen throws itself in front of an attack, negating it.

**Tier-III behavior:** This is the first fight where **PC death is expected** — run it that
way (the Ember Clock should be low; see death rules). On 0 HP with pyres down: the kingdom
shatters, the great hoard is freed, but the loose embers stream to the failing Source and
Halthurax rides them up. Final molt.

## FORM III → FORM IV — Halthurax, the Becoming God (Tier IV finale, effective CR ~26, phased)
**Base:** Ancient Gold Dragon + Dracolich, now **enthroned in a plane that is part of his
body.** Run as a **three-phase** battle. He has no normal "death" — instead he has a
**Hoard Pool** of **180 points** representing the souls still bound into him. The party wins
by emptying the Hoard Pool, not by HP alone.

**Reducing the Hoard Pool:** each phase, the party can drain the Pool by (a) destroying a
**Choir-anchor** (3 per phase, AC 19, 50 HP each — each destroyed releases ~20 Pool), (b)
*convincing or releasing* hoarded souls via skill challenges or spells (DC 20, releases ~10),
or (c) spending a freed legacy-item capstone power. **The villain cannot be reduced below 1
HP while the Hoard Pool is above 0.**

- **Phase 1 (Hoard Pool 180–121):** statline as Tier III, +50 HP, breath recharges 4–6. The
  Choir of the Hoarded grants him advantage on all saves.
- **Phase 2 (120–61):** he sheds the dragon shape partway — gains a second breath weapon
  (radiant "false dawn," DC 23, 18d6, blinds on a fail) and a third legendary action,
  **Unmake**, forcing a single creature to make a DC 23 Con save or drop to 0 HP (a death
  save, not death) as he tries to "save" them into the hoard.
- **Phase 3 (60–0, Hoard Pool emptying):** desperate and *pleading* — this is where the
  thesis scene can interrupt the fight (he offers the bargain mid-battle). If refused, he
  fights with everything; on Pool 0 and 0 HP he is **finally, permanently slain**, and the
  embers he hoarded return to the Source (or scatter, depending on the Ember Clock and the
  party's final choices — see endings, §6 of the main doc).

**Tuning note:** at a 20th-level party of 4–5 PCs with capstone legacy items, this is meant to
be *survivable but lethal* — expect at least one PC to fall. That fallen PC is exactly who the
"return at the Source" grace note in the endings is built for.

## Roleplaying Halthurax across all forms
- **He never gloats.** He grieves. His threat is conviction, not malice.
- **He remembers being good**, and he'll remind the party that he held this duty for a
  thousand years before they were born.
- **He offers off-ramps** every tier — stop, join him, let him save them. The horror is that
  some of it is true.
- **His tell that he's wrong:** he can never quite answer *what a world with no new souls
  becomes.* Let a clever party corner him on it. That's the dagger.
