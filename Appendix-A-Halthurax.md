# APPENDIX A — Halthurax, the Goldwyrm Who Would Not Burn
### The recurring villain's four-form stat progression

The party fights Halthurax once per tier, and each time he meets them as a more-complete
being. He can only truly be killed in Tier IV. Rather than give you four whole stat blocks to
manage, these notes are written as deltas off existing 5e 2014 monsters, so you can run him at
the table without inventing anything: take the base creature, apply the changes listed, and
drop in the Hoard Phylactery rule.

> The Hoard Phylactery (universal rule, all forms). Halthurax's phylactery is the mass of
> embers he has hoarded across the planes. As long as even one tier's hoard remains unfreed,
> dropping his current form to 0 HP only destroys that form rather than killing him — he
> reconstitutes in the next plane after the tier ends. He is permanently slain only in Tier IV,
> and only once the party has reduced his hoard pool (see Form IV) to 0.

---

## FORM 0 — The Living Wyrm (Tier I sighting only, ~CR 17)

Base: Adult or Ancient Gold Dragon (use the Ancient Gold Dragon from the MM, run as written).

Role: this is not a fight. He appears for one scene at the bottom of the Marrowmoor Vault, vast
and golden and sad. He speaks, warns the party off for their own good, and leaves. If the
players attack, he doesn't kill them; he restrains them, lectures them, and departs, which is
far more unsettling than a slaughter would be. The scene establishes that the monster was the
protector, and that he is not cruel.

The Herald (Tier I actual boss, CR ~3) is Halthurax's mortal agent. Base: Cult Fanatic (MM)
with these changes. HP becomes 65. He gains Touch of the Hoard, a melee spell attack at +5
dealing 3d6 necrotic, which also reduces the target's hit point maximum by the damage dealt
until a long rest. He commands 2d4 skeletons or zombies and one wight. Killing the Herald frees
the Marrowmoor hoard and triggers Halthurax's first molt.

## FORM I → FORM II — The Dragon-Wraith (Tier II boss, CR ~13)

Base: Adult Gold Dragon (MM), then apply the wraith-dragon template described below.

- Type becomes Undead. He gains resistance to necrotic, acid, and cold; no vulnerabilities; and
  immunity to poison, exhaustion, the frightened condition, and the charmed condition.
- Incorporeal Movement (as the Wraith): he can move through creatures and objects, taking 1d10
  force damage if he ends his turn inside one.
- Replace the fire breath with Graying Breath (Recharge 5–6): a 60-ft cone, DC 17 Con save,
  10d8 necrotic on a failure (half on a save). Each creature that fails also has its hit point
  maximum reduced by that amount until a long rest. Plants and fey light in the area wither.
- Soul Siphon (1/turn, free): when he reduces a creature to 0 HP, Halthurax gains temporary HP
  equal to half the creature's level or CR times 5, and that creature's mote is hoarded unless
  it is freed.
- Legendary Resistance (2/day) and 3 legendary actions: Ashen Gaze (1; DC 16 Wis or disadvantage
  on its next attack), Cinder Lash (1; tail +10, 2d8+6 plus 1d6 necrotic), and Wing of Ash (2;
  DC 16 Dex, 2d6 plus prone, fills 15 ft with gray fog).
- Lair actions (Pyre's Heart, initiative 20): Drown the Light (gutter a relit fount), The Graying
  (20-ft ash radius, 1d10 necrotic plus difficult terrain), and Hollow Bloom (DC 14 Dex or
  restrained). Full flavor text is on the Boss One-Sheet (`one-sheets/Boss-One-Sheet.md`).

Tier-II behavior: first comes a hunt set-piece at level 9, where he stalks, harries, and
retreats but can't be killed yet because it isn't time. Then comes the real fight at the Pyre's
Heart at level 10. At 0 HP his form is destroyed, the hoard is partly freed, and he molts toward
the Shadowfell.

## FORM II → FORM III — Halthurax the Dracolich (Tier III boss, CR ~21)

Base: an Adult Gold Dragon statline scaled up with the Dracolich template (see MToF or the
classic dracolich rules), or build off the Ancient Gold Dragon with these changes.

- Type becomes Undead. He is immune to poison and necrotic damage and to the charmed,
  frightened, paralyzed, poisoned, and exhaustion conditions. He resists cold and lightning, as
  well as bludgeoning, piercing, and slashing from nonmagical attacks.
- Legendary Resistance (3/day).
- Distributed phylactery: while in his Hoard-Kingdom, he regains 30 HP at the start of each of
  his turns unless his throne-vault's Soul-Pyres (3 of them, AC 17, 60 HP each, scattered around
  the room) have all been destroyed. This forces the party to split their focus between the
  dragon and the pyres, and it's the first time their choices can actually stop his regeneration.
- Breath — Hoardfire (Recharge 5–6): a 90-ft cone, DC 22 Dex save, 16d6 necrotic plus fire, half
  on a save. A creature reduced to 0 by it is claimed, and its death save failures count double
  until it is freed.
- Lair actions (Ossuara throne-vault, initiative 20): The Floor Remembers (birth 2d6 skeletons or
  1d4 wights), Portcullis of Ribs (a bone grate, AC 17, 40 HP, seals a 10-ft passage), The Loving
  Dead (a 20-ft cube of grasping citizens, DC 18 Str or restrained), and Pyre-Light (a surviving
  Soul-Pyre flares and he regains 30 HP).
- 3 legendary actions: Tally the Hoard (1; locate every creature, ignoring unseen ones),
  Sovereign's Tail (1; +14, 2d8+8, halves the target's speed), and Call the Grateful Dead (2; a
  hoarded citizen bodyblocks an attack, negating it).

Tier-III behavior: this is the first fight where PC death is expected, so run it that way (the
Ember Clock should be low by now; see the death rules). When he drops to 0 HP with the pyres
down, the kingdom shatters and the great hoard is freed, but the loose embers stream to the
failing Source and Halthurax rides them up. This is his final molt.

## FORM III → FORM IV — Halthurax, the Becoming God (Tier IV finale, effective CR ~26, phased)

Base: Ancient Gold Dragon plus Dracolich, now enthroned in a plane that is part of his own body.
Run this as a three-phase battle. He has no normal death; instead he has a Hoard Pool of 180
points, representing the souls still bound into him. The party wins by emptying the Hoard Pool,
not by HP alone.

Reducing the Hoard Pool: each phase, the party can drain the Pool three ways. They can destroy a
Choir-anchor (3 per phase, AC 19, 50 HP each, with each one releasing about 20 Pool); they can
convince or release hoarded souls through skill challenges or spells (DC 20, releasing about 10);
or they can spend a freed legacy-item capstone power. Halthurax cannot be reduced below 1 HP
while the Hoard Pool is above 0.

- Phase 1 (Hoard Pool 180–121): use the Tier III statline with +50 HP, and his breath recharges
  on 4–6. The Choir of the Hoarded grants him advantage on all saves.
- Phase 2 (120–61): he sheds the dragon shape partway. He gains a second breath weapon, a radiant
  "false dawn" (DC 23, 18d6, blinds on a failure), and a third legendary action, Unmake, which
  forces a single creature to make a DC 23 Con save or drop to 0 HP (a death save, not death) as
  he tries to "save" them into the hoard.
- Phase 3 (60–0, Hoard Pool emptying): now he is desperate and pleading, and this is where the
  thesis scene can interrupt the fight as he offers the bargain mid-battle. If the party refuses,
  he fights with everything he has. At Pool 0 and 0 HP he is finally and permanently slain, and
  the embers he hoarded return to the Source (or scatter, depending on the Ember Clock and the
  party's final choices; see the endings in §6 of the main doc).

Legendary actions (3/round): Behold the Door (1; DC 23 Wis or no reactions until its next turn),
Sunlit Slam (1; +15, 3d10+9 bludgeoning plus 2d6 radiant), Unmake (2, Phase 2 onward; DC 23 Con
or drop to 0 HP), and Chorus Swell (2; advantage on saves plus reknit a Choir-anchor for 25 HP).

Lair actions (the Emberthrone, initiative 20): False Dawn (DC 20 Con, 3d6 radiant plus blinded),
Gravity of the Source (DC 20 Str or dragged 15 ft plus prone), Remembered Grief (DC 20 Wis or
frightened), and The Choir Answers (while any anchor stands, the Hoard Pool regains 10). Full
flavor text is on `one-sheets/Boss-One-Sheet.md`.

Tuning note: for a 20th-level party of 4–5 PCs with capstone legacy items, this fight is meant to
be survivable but lethal, so expect at least one PC to fall. That fallen PC is exactly who the
"return at the Source" grace note in the endings is built for.

## Roleplaying Halthurax across all forms

- He never gloats; he grieves. His threat comes from conviction, not malice.
- He remembers being good, and he'll remind the party that he held this duty for a thousand
  years before any of them were born.
- He offers off-ramps every tier — stop, join him, let him save you. The horror is that some of
  what he says is true.
- His tell that he's wrong is that he can never quite answer what a world with no new souls
  actually becomes. Let a clever party corner him on it; that's the dagger.
