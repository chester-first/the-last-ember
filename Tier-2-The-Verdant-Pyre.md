# TIER II — THE VERDANT PYRE
### A fully runnable arc for levels 5–10 (D&D 5e 2014) — the Feywild

The party crosses into the **Feywild**, where soul-fire blooms as living light — and finds
it graying. Halthurax, now a **Dragon-Wraith**, is blighting the wellspring of vitality to
fuel his ascent. This is the tier where the **Emberwardens (Conflict #1)** become central:
their fey chapter, the **Court of First Light**, is splitting, and the party's deeds tip it.

**Runs ~6–9 sessions.** Ends with the party at level 10, the Feywild healed or scarred, and
Halthurax's molt into the dracolich.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet

- **Where it goes:** the Crossing → Glimmermarch (hub) → the Graying Glades → the Court of
  First Light (the faction tier) → the Tallymasters' Bargain (social fork) → the Dragon-Wraith
  hunt → the Pyre's Heart (boss).
- **Three must-happen beats:** (1) the **Court meter locks** here — Keepers vs. Tallymasters
  baseline for the whole campaign; (2) the party is offered the **Tallymasters' Bargain** (real
  power for real corruption); (3) **Sera's Tether drops** as Halthurax's pull strengthens, and
  she can confront a piece of her past.
- **Legacy items hit BURNING** at the start of this tier.
- **Ember Clock:** drops to 11→10 across the tier (one auto at tier start for the Dimming, plus
  scatter ticks). At Clock 8 the "Dimming shows" threshold turns on — resurrection now costs an
  extra Ember segment.

### Level pacing
| Reach level | After… |
|---|---|
| 6 | The Crossing + first Graying Glade |
| 7–8 | The Court of First Light + the Bargain |
| 9 | The Dragon-Wraith hunt |
| 10 | The Pyre's Heart (boss) |

## 1. THE CROSSING & GLIMMERMARCH (hub)

Sera's pull (or Warden Greel's last map) leads to a **fey crossing** in the deep Marrowmoor
woods — a ring of birches where the light falls *upward*. Through it: the **Verdant Pyre**, a
Feywild realm where every living thing trails soul-fire like pollen. It should be the most
beautiful place the party has ever seen — and at the edges, it's going gray.

**Read aloud:**
> "You step between two birches and the world inhales. Color floods in — grass that glows from
> within, rivers of light running uphill, motes of gold drifting off every leaf like the whole
> place is gently burning and loving it. And then you see the far ridge, where the gold has gone
> the gray of old ash, and the burning has stopped, and nothing drifts at all."

**Glimmermarch** is the hub — a fey market-town built in the boughs of a single mile-wide tree,
the **Hearthtree**. Here the party meets the Court and the factions.

### NPC roster — Tier II
| NPC | Role | Want | Hook |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lady Aurene Firstlight** | Archfey, the Keeper faction's heart | Restore the old way — return the dead | The redemption path of Conflict #1; gives the Pyre's Heart quest |
| **Magister Vol the Tallymaster** | Archfey, leads the hoarders | "Mercy": keep souls safe from the Dimming | Offers the **Bargain**; the corruption path; secretly half-convinced by Halthurax |
| **Pip Emberkin** | A pixie scout, comic + brave | To matter | The party's guide; tracks the Dragon-Wraith; can die memorably |
| **Sera Vant** (returning) | The revenant ally | To stay herself | Her old knight-order had a fey patron *here*; Tether beats land in §4 |
| **The Gray Stag** | A blighted fey-beast, once the realm's guardian | Release | A mercy-or-mercy-kill set-piece (§2); foreshadows Ossuara's "grateful dead" |

**The Court is already split** when the party arrives. Aurene (Keepers) wants to heal the wound;
Vol (Tallymasters) wants to *evacuate* souls into a hoard "until the danger passes" — which is
Halthurax's exact logic wearing a kinder face. **Everything the party does in this tier tips the
Court meter.**

## 2. THE GRAYING GLADES (levels 5–6)

Three glades where the blight has taken hold. Each is a self-contained problem; running two
reaches level 6–7. Each glade offers a **return-vs-scatter** moral beat scaled up from Tier I.

### Glade of the Gray Stag (signature set-piece)
The realm's guardian-beast, the **Gray Stag**, has been half-drained — a noble creature now
caught between life and wraith. **Encounter (level 5–6):**
- **Gray Stag** — base **Cervine guardian**: use a buffed **Unicorn** (MM, CR 5) with the
  wraith template-lite (necrotic resistance, no healing aura, *Graying Touch* instead of horn:
  3d6 necrotic + max-HP reduction). HP 90. It is *suffering*, not evil.
- **2 blighted dryads** (use **Dryad**, MM, with necrotic damage swapped in) defend it,
  believing they're protecting it.
**The choice:** *scatter* the Stag (kill it — fast, the dryads scatter too, the glade dies) or
*free* it — a skill challenge (3 successes before 3 failures, DC 14, using the bond-light of
legacy items or a Burning *return* power) to release its hoarded fire. Freeing it heals the
glade and earns **Court meter +2 (Keepers)** and Aurene's open favor.

### Glade of Hollow Bloom (combat-forward)
A grove where the flowers have turned to ash-wraiths. **Encounter (level 6):** **4 specters**
+ **2 will-o'-wisps** + a **wraith** (MM, CR 5) "gardener." Straightforward fight; the wraith
drops a **Glimmer-seed** (treasure: plant it later to heal one blighted area / Ember Clock soft
reprieve).

### Glade of the Drowned Lantern (exploration/puzzle)
A flooded glade where a sunken Emberwarden shrine still holds a pocket of pure soul-fire the
blight can't cross. Inside: lore (the **second gold scale-shard** proving the Court that
Halthurax leads the Dimming) and the **Cinderheart** (a Tier II magic item — see treasure).

## 3. THE COURT OF FIRST LIGHT — the faction tier (levels 7–8)

This is the heart of Tier II and of **Conflict #1.** The Court convenes in the Hearthtree to
decide the realm's response to the blight. The party is invited to speak — and to *act* before
the vote.

### The Court meter, made concrete here
Across Tiers I–IV the **Wardens/Court meter** runs Keepers (+) ↔ Tallymasters (−). It entered
Tier II at its Tier I baseline. The following lock it for the rest of the campaign:
- **Keepers (+):** freeing the Gray Stag, returning motes, refusing the Bargain (§3.2), siding
  with Aurene in the vote, healing glades.
- **Tallymasters (−):** taking the Bargain, scattering for speed, siding with Vol, using hoarded
  fey-light to power up.

### 3.1 The Vote (a social set-piece, no combat)
A structured debate. The party makes the case to swing fence-sitting fey (run as a series of
**Persuasion/Insight/History DC 15–17** checks, weighted heavily by what they *did* in the
glades — actions beat words). The outcome sets the **Court's allegiance** that pays off in Tier
IV: a redeemed Court arrives as an army of light at the Emberthrone; a corrupted Court arrives
agreeing with Halthurax.

### 3.2 The Tallymasters' Bargain (the corruption fork — REQUIRED offer)
Magister Vol takes the party aside with a genuine, tempting offer:
> "You're going to fight a dragon. You'll lose, as you are. I can change that. We've gathered
> light — *saved* it, from souls that would only have been wasted returning. Take it. Grow.
> Burn brighter than you've any right to. It costs you nothing. It costs *them* nothing — they're
> already gone." He smiles, and means it. "Your enemy and I disagree about everything except
> this: the dead are more useful kept."

**Mechanically:** accepting grants each PC a real boon — choose one: **+1 to an ability score
(max 20)**, OR an **extra legacy-item Burning power early**, OR a pool of **40 temp HP per long
rest for the tier.** The cost: **Court meter −3 (Tallymasters)**, **Ember Clock −1**, and the
party has now *personally hoarded souls* — Halthurax notes it and treats them, in Tier III, as
fellow-travelers rather than fools. Refusing is harder and slower and *right.* **Do not punish
acceptance with a gotcha** — let it be real power that costs the world, exactly the campaign's
question in the players' own hands.

## 4. SERA'S TETHER (Conflict #3 beats this tier)

Halthurax's ascent pulls Sera harder in the Feywild (closer to the soul-stream). Two beats:
- **The Patron's Grave (Tether ±):** Sera's old knight-order served a fey lord *here*, now
  blighted. The party can help her give him a proper return — a quiet, non-combat scene. Doing
  it well (honoring her grief, not rushing) restores **Tether +2**. Skipping or scattering the
  patron costs **−1**.
- **The Pull (automatic −1):** at the tier's midpoint Sera loses a memory to Halthurax — she
  forgets a name, a face. Roleplay it; it's the cost of the campaign moving forward. Players who
  invested in her will feel it.

End-of-tier Tether is carried into the Shadowfell, where her original liege waits in Ossuara.

## 5. THE DRAGON-WRAITH HUNT (level 9)

Before the boss, Halthurax's second form **hunts the party** through the dimming Feywild — a
chase/skirmish that establishes he can't yet be killed, only driven off.

**Run as a 3-stage pursuit:**
1. **The Stalk** — Pip Emberkin spots him circling; the party flees toward the Pyre's Heart
   across collapsing fey terrain (group Athletics/Acrobatics DC 14 hazards).
2. **The Strike** — the Dragon-Wraith (Appendix A, Form II, CR 13) dives once: a single round
   of his **Graying Breath**, then he passes through a hill and is gone. Sell the terror; this
   is a taste, not the fight.
3. **The Cost** — he claims someone or something — ideally **Pip** dies buying the party time
   (a willing, heroic NPC death that makes the boss personal), or a PC takes a lasting max-HP
   reduction. Let the loss land.

## 6. THE PYRE'S HEART — tier boss (level 10)

The wound in the Feywild — a guttering well of soul-fire the Dragon-Wraith is draining. Healing
it is the goal; the dragon-wraith is the obstacle.

**The arena:** a vast inverted crater of gray ash around a dimming core of gold. **Three
Pyre-Founts** (AC 15, 40 HP each) ring the core — relit, each grants the party advantage on
saves vs. the Graying Breath in its radius and weakens the wraith. The party must fight *and*
tend the founts: a dynamic objective fight, not a slugfest.

**THE DRAGON-WRAITH** — full block in `Appendix-A-Halthurax.md` (Form II, CR ~13). Key beats:
- **Graying Breath (recharge 5–6):** the signature max-HP-reduction cone. The relit founts are
  the counterplay.
- **Soul Siphon:** he heals off anyone he drops — protect the fallen.
- **Court reinforcements:** if the party redeemed the Court, **Aurene and 4 fey knights** arrive
  at initiative 10 to help. If corrupted, the party fights alone (or worse, Vol's hoarders watch
  and do nothing). *This is the first payoff of Conflict #1 in a fight.*

**On 0 HP:** the wraith-form is destroyed, the Pyre's Heart blazes back to gold, and the Feywild
**heals visibly** — a huge, earned win. But the freed motes pour toward the failing Source, and
Halthurax rides them down into the **Shadowfell**, completing his lichdom. Final molt of the tier.

---

## 7. AFTERMATH & HOOKS TO TIER III

- **Party hits level 10.** Carry forward: Ember Clock, the **locked Court allegiance**, Sera's
  Tether. **Legacy items reach BLAZING** at the start of Tier III.
- **The Feywild remembers.** Whether brightened (Keepers) or hollowed (Tallymasters), the realm's
  state is now a standing testament to the party's choices — NPCs reference it for the rest of
  the campaign.
- **The way down.** The freed motes tore a **soul-fall** into the Shadowfell. Sera, pulled
  hardest of all, knows the way — and dreads it, because her old liege rules down there now.
- **Two scale-shards in hand.** The party now has proof of Halthurax's identity to wield in the
  political tier ahead.
