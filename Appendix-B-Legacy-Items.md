# APPENDIX B — Ember-Forged Legacy Items
### The bonded item subsystem (one per PC, grows across all 20 levels)

5e 2014 has no native "growing item" rules, so this is a clean, self-contained subsystem.
Each PC bonds **one** item at level 1 that holds a mote of their own soul-fire. It grows at
**fixed campaign milestones**, never randomly — the same soul-vessel mechanic Halthurax
abuses, used rightly. The difference between the heroes and the villain is the whole point:
**theft vs. consent.**

---

## How it works

- **Bonding (Tier I, the barrow-altar).** Each player picks an object meaningful to their PC
  — a weapon, a holy symbol, an heirloom, an instrument, a shield, a focus. At the altar it
  drinks a mote of their fire and wakes. From now on it is **attuned permanently and freely**
  (doesn't count against the 3-attunement limit), and it **cannot be sundered or lost while
  the PC lives** — it reappears in hand after a long rest.
- **Milestones (fixed).** The item gains a new tier of power at these story beats, matching the
  legacy-item milestones in the main doc:
  - **Tier I — Kindled** (level 1, at bonding)
  - **Tier II — Burning** (start of levels 5–10 arc)
  - **Tier III — Blazing** (start of levels 11–16 arc)
  - **Tier IV — Radiant** (start of levels 17–20 arc)
  - **Capstone — the True Ember** (campaign's end, surviving PCs only)
- **The dark mirror.** Each item has a **Hoarding temptation** built in: at any milestone a
  PC may "feed" the item a *stolen* mote (scatter instead of return; accept a Tallymaster
  shortcut) to unlock that tier's power *one tier early or stronger.* Doing so ticks Conflict
  #1 toward Corrupt and the Ember Clock down. The item literally offers the player Halthurax's
  bargain, in miniature, four times.

---

## Build-your-own framework (recommended)

Rather than fixed items, build each PC's legacy item from a **theme** + a **power budget per
tier.** Pick one theme per PC; grant the listed power at each milestone. Keep parity by giving
every item roughly the same budget.

### Power budget per milestone
| Milestone | Roughly equivalent to | Bonus to attacks/save DC (if a weapon/focus) |
|---|---|---|
| Kindled (1) | a *common*/minor uncommon item; one signature trick | — |
| Burning (5) | +1 item + one active power (recharge/short rest) | +1 |
| Blazing (11) | +2 item + a strong active + a passive | +2 |
| Radiant (17) | +3 item + a once/day big effect + situational immunity | +3 |
| True Ember (cap) | legendary capstone; a 1/campaign reality-bending act | +3 |

### Six ready-made themes (one per common PC archetype)

**1. The Pyre-Blade (martial weapon).**
- *Kindled:* deals +1d4 fire; sheds light 10 ft you control.
- *Burning:* +1; once per short rest, a hit **returns** a slain undead's mote (free, no
  scatter) and heals you 1d8.
- *Blazing:* +2; on a crit, 3d6 radiant and the target can't regain HP until your next turn
  (wrecks regenerating undead — and Halthurax's pyres).
- *Radiant:* +3; 1/day **Dawnstroke** — one attack deals an extra 8d6 radiant and, vs.
  undead, forces a DC 18 Con save or be destroyed if under half HP.
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, you may **return a fallen ally's mote** at the moment of their
  death, restoring them to 1 HP — once.

**2. The Wardens' Aegis (shield/armor).**
- *Kindled:* +1 AC vs. undead; advantage on saves vs. being frightened.
- *Burning:* +1 shield; reaction once/short rest to halve necrotic damage to an ally within 5 ft.
- *Blazing:* +2; aura (10 ft) grants allies resistance to the **hit-point-maximum-reduction**
  effects that define this campaign's undead.
- *Radiant:* +3; 1/day raise a 15-ft **Ember Bulwark** (total cover wall, 1 min) that returns
  any undead mote passing through it.
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, **negate one creature's death entirely** within 30 ft (ally or
  the villain's Unmake), no save.

**3. The Soul-Lantern (cleric/paladin holy focus).**
- *Kindled:* as a focus; *light* at will, and you always know if undead are within 30 ft.
- *Burning:* spell save DC +1; once/short rest, **return** a destroyed undead's mote and grant
  an ally temp HP = your level.
- *Blazing:* +2; your Channel Divinity / Lay on Hands also heals **the Ember Clock pressure** —
  narratively staves off the Dimming in a region (GM: worth a soft Clock reprieve).
- *Radiant:* +3; 1/day call a column of true dawn — 6th-level *sunbeam* that *returns* every
  mote it destroys.
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, **open a path to the Source** — the act that lets a permanently-dead
  PC be restored in Tier IV.

**4. The Gravesong (bard/rogue instrument or blade).**
- *Kindled:* you can speak with the recently dead (1/day, as *speak with dead* lite).
- *Burning:* +1; once/short rest, **soothe** a hoarded soul or undead into standing down
  (Wis save DC = your spell DC) instead of fighting — pure non-combat resolution power.
- *Blazing:* +2; advantage on all social checks with the dead and the fey of the Verdant Pyre;
  learn one secret from any soul you return.
- *Radiant:* +3; 1/day **Chorus of Release** — up to 6 undead/hoarded souls within 30 ft make a
  save or are peacefully returned (huge in Tier IV's Choir).
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, **turn one of Halthurax's lieutenants** (even Sera, if defected)
  back to your side for the finale.

**5. The Wyrmscale Token (sorcerer/draconic, or any caster).**
- *Kindled:* resistance to one damage type tied to your PC's "dragon" (player picks).
- *Burning:* +1 spell attack; once/short rest add 1d8 of your type to a spell.
- *Blazing:* +2; 1/day breathe a 30-ft cone of your element (8d6) — a nod to the dragon war
  underneath the campaign.
- *Radiant:* +3; you gain a flying speed and **frightful presence** vs. undead.
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, **briefly assume the form of a true gold dragon** (as Halthurax
  *was*) — the heroic answer to his fall.

**6. The Quiet Heirloom (any PC; the "non-combatant's" item).**
- *Kindled:* once/day reroll a failed save and keep the new roll; the item warms when a loved
  NPC is in danger.
- *Burning:* advantage on checks to **sway the Emberwardens** (Conflict #1) and to **steady
  Sera's Tether** (Conflict #3) — mechanizes the social spine.
- *Blazing:* 1/day, grant an ally inspiration *and* immunity to fear for a scene.
- *Radiant:* 1/day, when an ally would drop to 0, they instead drop to half (the heirloom
  "remembers" them).
- *True Ember:* 1/campaign, **preserve one mote from the Dimming forever** — guarantees one
  NPC the party loves gets a future, whatever ending they earn.

---

## GM guidance

- **Keep parity.** If one player wants something custom, match it to the per-milestone budget
  table — don't let one item outshine the rest.
- **Tie the item to the PC's arc, not just their class.** The best version of this subsystem is
  when the *Burning* power a player unlocks comments on a choice they made the previous tier.
- **The Hoarding temptation is the heart of it.** Offer the dark-mirror upgrade at each
  milestone *by name.* Some players will take it. That's not a trap — it's the campaign asking
  its central question through their own hands. Track it; it pays off in §6's endings.
- **Capstones are once-per-campaign on purpose.** They are designed to be spent in Tier IV, and
  several of them (Pyre-Blade, Soul-Lantern, Aegis, Gravesong) are literally the tools that
  resolve the finale and the death arc. Don't let players forget they have them.
