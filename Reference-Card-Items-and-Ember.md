## Your theme's track

Find your item's theme below and copy its five powers into the milestone slots on your card. A "+X" is the bonus to your attack and save DC if the item is a weapon or focus.

### Pyre-Blade — a martial weapon
- Kindled: +1d4 fire on hits; sheds 10 ft of light you control.
- Burning (+1): once per short rest, a hit that drops an undead returns its soul and heals you 1d8.
- Blazing (+2): on a crit, +3d6 radiant, and the target can't regain HP until your next turn.
- Radiant (+3): 1/day Dawnstroke — one attack does +8d6 radiant, and undead under half HP must save (DC 18 Con) or be destroyed.
- True Ember: 1/campaign, catch a falling ally's soul the instant they die, bringing them back at 1 HP.

### Wardens' Aegis — a shield or armor
- Kindled: +1 AC against undead; advantage on saves against fear.
- Burning (+1 shield): as a reaction once per short rest, halve the necrotic damage to an ally within 5 ft.
- Blazing (+2): allies within 10 ft resist the soul-draining, max-HP-loss effects that define this campaign's undead.
- Radiant (+3): 1/day, raise a 15-ft Ember Bulwark wall (total cover, 1 minute).
- True Ember: 1/campaign, cancel one creature's death within 30 ft — no save.

### Soul-Lantern — a holy symbol or focus
- Kindled: cast *light* at will; you always sense undead within 30 ft.
- Burning (save DC +1): once per short rest, return a destroyed undead's soul and grant an ally temp HP equal to your level.
- Blazing (+2): your healing pushes back the Dimming in a region (ask your GM).
- Radiant (+3): 1/day, call a column of true dawn (*sunbeam*) that returns every soul it destroys.
- True Ember: 1/campaign, open a path to the Source — the way home for the truly lost.

### Gravesong — an instrument or a rogue's blade
- Kindled: speak with the recently dead, 1/day.
- Burning (+1): once per short rest, talk down an undead or restless soul (Wis save) instead of fighting it.
- Blazing (+2): advantage with the dead and the fey; learn a secret from any soul you return.
- Radiant (+3): 1/day, Chorus of Release — up to 6 souls within 30 ft save or peacefully return.
- True Ember: 1/campaign, turn one enemy lieutenant back to your side for one battle.

### Wyrmscale Token — a draconic relic (any caster)
- Kindled: resistance to one damage type (your "dragon" — you pick).
- Burning (+1 spell attack): once per short rest, add 1d8 of your type to a spell.
- Blazing (+2): 1/day, breathe a 30-ft cone of your element (8d6).
- Radiant (+3): gain a flying speed and frightful presence against undead.
- True Ember: 1/campaign, briefly become a true gold dragon — the hero's answer to the fall.

### Quiet Heirloom — for the PC who'd rather not fight
- Kindled: 1/day, reroll a failed save and keep the new roll; the item warms when a loved one is in danger.
- Burning: advantage on checks to sway people's loyalties and steady fraying bonds.
- Blazing: 1/day, grant an ally inspiration and immunity to fear for a scene.
- Radiant: 1/day, when an ally would hit 0 HP, they drop to half instead.
- True Ember: 1/campaign, guarantee one person you love a future, whatever ending comes.

## The Ember Clock — the world's remaining soul-fire (shared)

The Ember Clock is a single track that runs from 12 down to 0. It only ever goes down. You can slow it, but you can't refill it until the very end — and only if you earn the right.

Every undead is a stolen soul. When you destroy one, you choose what happens to it:

- Return it. Spend a moment on it — an action and a small check — and the soul goes home. This slows the Clock. It's the kind, harder way.
- Scatter it. Just kill it. It's fast, but the soul is lost forever, and it pushes the Clock down. The quick, costly way.

Here is what you'll feel as the Clock drains:

| Clock | What it means |
|---|---|
| 12–9 | The world is just "off." Coming back from death works normally. |
| 8–5 | The Dimming shows. Raising the dead costs more — a piece of this very Clock. |
| 4–2 | Crisis. Coming back may take someone else's soul, or you come back Touched: changed, marked, not quite who you were. |
| 1–0 | No easy way back remains. The only road home is at the Source. |

Death is part of this story, and coming back is never free. That's the game.
