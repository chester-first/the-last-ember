# TIER IV — THE EMBERTHRONE
### A fully runnable arc for levels 17–20 (D&D 5e 2014) — the Source

The party climbs the soul-stream against the current to the **Emberthrone** — a plane Halthurax
is *building* around the dying Source so that he becomes the gate every soul must pass through.
Everything pays off here: the Court arrives as army or enemy, the Ember Clock decides whether the
Source can be refilled, Sera's fate lands, the legacy items hit their **capstone**, and the
campaign's question — *is death worth keeping?* — gets its answer.

**Runs ~5–8 sessions.** Ends the campaign. **Player death is expected; so is grace.**

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet

- **Where it goes:** the Ascent → the Hollow Heaven (the half-built plane) → the Choir of the
  Hoarded → the Two Endings of Death (thesis scene) → the Becoming God (final, phased boss) →
  the chosen ending.
- **Three must-happen beats:** (1) the **Choir** — unmaking the last hoard means releasing souls
  dead a thousand years; (2) the **thesis scene**, where Halthurax offers a *genuine* bargain the
  party can take; (3) the **final battle**, won by emptying his Hoard Pool, not just his HP.
- **Legacy items hit RADIANT** at tier start; **TRUE EMBER capstones** unlock for the finale.
- **Ember Clock:** at 1–0 no natural resurrection remains — but the Source itself can restore the
  fallen *here*, the one place the Dimming can be undone.

### Level pacing
| Reach level | After… |
|---|---|
| 18 | The Ascent + the Hollow Heaven |
| 19 | The Choir + the thesis scene |
| 20 | The Becoming God (finale) |

## 1. THE ASCENT & THE HOLLOW HEAVEN (levels 17–18)

The soul-stream runs upward now. Climbing it is the first trial — a plane that is *part of
Halthurax's body*, rewriting itself to keep them out.

**Read aloud:**
> "You climb a river of souls falling upward. At the top, light — but wrong light, too even, too
> kept. A heaven with no horizon, built room by room around a single failing flame the size of a
> sun gone to embers. This is the Source. And around it, scaffolding of gold and bone, half a
> cathedral, half a *throat* — Halthurax building the door he means to make every soul pass
> through. He is so close to finished. He is so glad you came."

**The Hollow Heaven** is the dungeon-plane. Hazards over fights: gravity that follows
Halthurax's will, rooms that *remember* the party's worst moments (Wisdom saves vs. their own
guilt — the Grateful Dead, Pip, the Bargain, any PC they failed to save), and the constant
upward pull toward the Source.

### Allies arrive (Conflict #1 pays off)
- **Redeemed Court:** Aurene leads an **army of First Light** that breaches the Hollow Heaven
  with the party — they hold the scaffolding while the party climbs. Mechanically: the party
  fights the finale *unharried* by adds.
- **Corrupted Court:** Vol's Tallymasters are *here too* — agreeing with Halthurax, fighting
  *for* him. The party faces a second enemy faction throughout the tier.

---

## 2. THE CHOIR OF THE HOARDED (level 18–19)

The assembled stolen souls — every mote Halthurax ever took, sung into a single chorused mind —
defend the Throne. To reach him the party must **unmake the hoard one last time**, which means
convincing or releasing souls who have been dead, safe, and *content* for a thousand years.

**Run as a grand skill challenge + set-piece:**
- **The Choir-anchors:** 3 great soul-anchors (AC 19, 50 HP each). Destroying one releases a
  swathe of the Choir.
- **The plea:** alternatively (and more in the campaign's spirit), the party can *release* souls
  by answering them — skill challenges where a redeemed Court, the Gravesong item's Chorus of
  Release, Sera's testimony, or a clever argument frees them peacefully (DC 20 checks).
- **The cost:** some souls *don't want to go.* Mother Cinder is here. The child is here. Freeing
  them is mercy that feels like cruelty — the campaign's thesis, one last time, in faces.

This directly drains Halthurax's **Hoard Pool** before the final fight (see Appendix A, Form IV)
— the more the party releases now, the more killable he is at the climax.

---

## 3. THE TWO ENDINGS OF DEATH (level 19 — the thesis scene)

Before or during the finale, Halthurax makes a **final, genuine bargain.** This is the campaign's
central question made playable. He is not lying.

> "One ritual. That's all that's left. Help me finish — or simply *stand aside* — and I will be
> the door, and no soul will ever again be unmade. Your dead. *Your* dead. The child. The pixie.
> The ones you've lost on the way up. I can keep them all. Whole. Forever. I am not asking you to
> serve me. I am asking you to let me *win,* and watch everyone you've ever buried open their
> eyes." He is weeping. "Tell me death is worth more than that. Tell me, and mean it, and I will
> believe you fought for something."

**The party can take it.** The campaign supports siding with him (§5). The honest counter — the
dagger — is the one thing he can never answer: *a world where no one dies is a world where no one
is born.* The Mercy of Never is a paradise that slowly empties of children, a kept and ending
world. Let a player put that to him. Let it cost them to choose it.

---

## 4. THE BECOMING GOD — the finale (level 20)

Halthurax at the lip of the Source, fighting with the **whole hoard as his hit points.** Won by
**emptying his Hoard Pool**, not HP alone. Full phased block in `Appendix-A-Halthurax.md`
(Form IV, effective CR ~26).

- **Phase 1 (Pool 180–121):** the dracolich enthroned, the Choir granting him advantage on saves.
  The party drains the Pool via Choir-anchors, releases, and capstone item powers.
- **Phase 2 (120–61):** he sheds the dragon shape; a radiant "false dawn" breath, and **Unmake**
  — he tries to "save" a PC into the hoard (DC 23 Con or drop to 0). The legacy-item capstones
  (Aegis's *cancel a death*, Pyre-Blade's *catch a falling ally*, Soul-Lantern's *path to the
  Source*) are built to answer exactly this.
- **Phase 3 (60–0, Pool emptying):** desperate, pleading, the thesis scene can interrupt the
  battle. On Pool 0 **and** 0 HP, he is **finally, permanently slain** — and the embers return to
  the Source, or scatter, per the chosen ending.

**Expect a PC to fall here.** That fallen PC is exactly who the Source's grace (§6) is built for.

## 5. THE THREE ENDINGS

Track which the party earns (full text in `The-Last-Ember.md` §6):

- **The Old Order Restored** — refuse him, refill the Source. Requires a healthy-enough Ember
  Clock and a redeemed Court. Death resumes its rightful, necessary work; the party's own dead
  stay dead, and that is the price of a living world. The hardest, truest ending.
- **The Pyrrhic Throne** — defeat him with a gutted Ember Clock. The world survives diminished, a
  long winter of scarcity. Victory that costs the future.
- **The Mercy of Never** — join him. No one ever dies again, including everyone the party loved —
  and the world slowly stops being born. A paradise with no children, ruled by a grieving god and
  his hero-lieutenants. Played straight, not punished with a gotcha.

### Sera's fate resolves (Conflict #3)
- **Freed (Tether 7+):** she returns her own mote willingly, at peace — the campaign's gentlest
  grace note. May reincarnate.
- **Martyr (Tether 3–6):** she spends her last self to open the path to the Source — the party's
  costliest ally, the reason a fallen PC can come home.
- **Defector (Tether 0–2):** she stands among Halthurax's lieutenants at the Throne. The party's
  one failure, made flesh, blade in hand.

## 6. CAPSTONE & GRACE

- **Survivors' capstones:** each surviving PC's legacy item reaches its **True Ember** form
  (Appendix B) — several are literally the tools that resolve the finale and the death arc.
- **The grace note:** PCs who **died and are returned at the Source** in this tier come back
  **whole** — not Touched, not diminished. The Emberthrone is the one place the Dimming can be
  undone. After twenty levels of death-with-teeth, the campaign earns the right to give one back
  clean.
- **Epilogue prompts (per ending):**
  - *Old Order:* what does each PC bury, now that burying means something again?
  - *Pyrrhic:* what does the long winter cost them, and who do they become in it?
  - *Mercy:* what is it like to be a hero-lieutenant of a kind, grieving, world-ending god?
