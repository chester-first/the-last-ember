# TIER IV — THE EMBERTHRONE
### A fully runnable arc for levels 17–20 (D&D 5e 2014) — the Source

The party climbs the soul-stream against the current to the Emberthrone — a plane Halthurax is building around the dying Source, so that he becomes the gate every soul must pass through. Everything pays off here. The Court arrives as army or enemy, the Ember Clock decides whether the Source can be refilled, Sera's fate lands, the legacy items hit their capstone, and the campaign's question — is death worth keeping? — finally gets its answer.

This arc runs about five to eight sessions, and it ends the campaign. Player death is expected here; so is grace.

---

## 0. GM cheat-sheet

Here is the shape of the finale. The party makes the Ascent, reaches the Hollow Heaven (the half-built plane), faces the Choir of the Hoarded, plays through the Two Endings of Death (the thesis scene), fights the Becoming God (the final, phased boss), and arrives at the chosen ending.

Three beats have to happen. First, the Choir: unmaking the last hoard means releasing souls dead a thousand years. Second, the thesis scene, where Halthurax offers a genuine bargain the party can take. Third, the final battle, which is won by emptying his Hoard Pool, not just his HP.

Two notes on power. Legacy items hit RADIANT at tier start, and TRUE EMBER capstones unlock for the finale. As for the Ember Clock: at 1 to 0 no natural resurrection remains, but the Source itself can restore the fallen here, the one place the Dimming can be undone.

## 1. THE ASCENT & THE HOLLOW HEAVEN (levels 17–18)

The soul-stream runs upward now. Climbing it is the first trial — a plane that is part of Halthurax's body, rewriting itself to keep them out.

Read aloud:
> "You climb a river of souls falling upward. At the top, light — but wrong light, too even, too kept. A heaven with no horizon, built room by room around a single failing flame the size of a sun gone to embers. This is the Source. And around it, scaffolding of gold and bone, half a cathedral, half a *throat* — Halthurax building the door he means to make every soul pass through. He is so close to finished. He is so glad you came."

The Hollow Heaven is the dungeon-plane, and it favors hazards over fights. Gravity follows Halthurax's will, rooms remember the party's worst moments (Wisdom saves against their own guilt — the Grateful Dead, Pip, the Bargain, any PC they failed to save), and there is a constant upward pull toward the Source.

### Allies arrive (Conflict #1 pays off)
How the Court turned out earlier decides what happens here.

- **Redeemed Court:** Aurene leads an army of First Light that breaches the Hollow Heaven alongside the party — they hold the scaffolding while the party climbs. Mechanically, the party fights the finale unharried by adds.
- **Corrupted Court:** Vol's Tallymasters are here too, agreeing with Halthurax and fighting for him. The party faces a second enemy faction throughout the tier.

---

## 2. THE CHOIR OF THE HOARDED (level 18–19)

The assembled stolen souls — every mote Halthurax ever took, sung into a single chorused mind — defend the Throne. To reach him, the party must unmake the hoard one last time, which means convincing or releasing souls who have been dead, safe, and content for a thousand years.

Run it as a grand skill challenge plus set-piece.

- **The Choir-anchors:** three great soul-anchors (AC 19, 50 HP each). Destroying one releases a swathe of the Choir.
- **The plea:** alternatively, and more in the campaign's spirit, the party can release souls by answering them — skill challenges where a redeemed Court, the Gravesong item's Chorus of Release, Sera's testimony, or a clever argument frees them peacefully (DC 20 checks).
- **The cost:** some souls don't want to go. Mother Cinder is here. The child is here. Freeing them is mercy that feels like cruelty — the campaign's thesis, one last time, in faces.

All of this directly drains Halthurax's Hoard Pool before the final fight (see Appendix A, Form IV). The more the party releases now, the more killable he is at the climax.

---

## 3. THE TWO ENDINGS OF DEATH (level 19 — the thesis scene)

Before or during the finale, Halthurax makes a final, genuine bargain. This is the campaign's central question made playable, and he is not lying.

> "One ritual. That's all that's left. Help me finish — or simply *stand aside* — and I will be the door, and no soul will ever again be unmade. Your dead. *Your* dead. The child. The pixie. The ones you've lost on the way up. I can keep them all. Whole. Forever. I am not asking you to serve me. I am asking you to let me *win,* and watch everyone you've ever buried open their eyes." He is weeping. "Tell me death is worth more than that. Tell me, and mean it, and I will believe you fought for something."

The party can take it. The campaign supports siding with him (§5). The honest counter — the dagger — is the one thing he can never answer: a world where no one dies is a world where no one is born. The Mercy of Never is a paradise that slowly empties of children, a kept and ending world. Let a player put that to him, and let it cost them to choose it.

---

## 4. THE BECOMING GOD — the finale (level 20)

Halthurax stands at the lip of the Source, fighting with the whole hoard as his hit points. He is won by emptying his Hoard Pool, not HP alone. The full phased block is in `Appendix-A-Halthurax.md` (Form IV, effective CR around 26).

- **Phase 1 (Pool 180–121):** the dracolich enthroned, the Choir granting him advantage on saves. The party drains the Pool via Choir-anchors, releases, and capstone item powers.
- **Phase 2 (120–61):** he sheds the dragon shape, breathes a radiant "false dawn," and uses Unmake — he tries to "save" a PC into the hoard (DC 23 Con or drop to 0). The legacy-item capstones are built to answer exactly this: Aegis can cancel a death, the Pyre-Blade can catch a falling ally, and the Soul-Lantern can open a path to the Source.
- **Phase 3 (60–0, Pool emptying):** desperate and pleading, he can have the thesis scene interrupt the battle. On Pool 0 and 0 HP, he is finally, permanently slain — and the embers return to the Source, or scatter, per the chosen ending.

Expect a PC to fall here. That fallen PC is exactly who the Source's grace (§6) is built for.

## 5. THE THREE ENDINGS

Track which one the party earns (full text in `The-Last-Ember.md` §6).

- **The Old Order Restored** — refuse him and refill the Source. This requires a healthy-enough Ember Clock and a redeemed Court. Death resumes its rightful, necessary work; the party's own dead stay dead, and that is the price of a living world. The hardest, truest ending.
- **The Pyrrhic Throne** — defeat him with a gutted Ember Clock. The world survives diminished, in a long winter of scarcity. Victory that costs the future.
- **The Mercy of Never** — join him. No one ever dies again, including everyone the party loved — and the world slowly stops being born. A paradise with no children, ruled by a grieving god and his hero-lieutenants. Play it straight, not punished with a gotcha.

### Sera's fate resolves (Conflict #3)
- **Freed (Tether 7+):** she returns her own mote willingly, at peace — the campaign's gentlest grace note. She may reincarnate.
- **Martyr (Tether 3–6):** she spends her last self to open the path to the Source — the party's costliest ally, and the reason a fallen PC can come home.
- **Defector (Tether 0–2):** she stands among Halthurax's lieutenants at the Throne. The party's one failure, made flesh, blade in hand.

## 6. CAPSTONE & GRACE

Each surviving PC's legacy item reaches its True Ember form (Appendix B). Several of these are literally the tools that resolve the finale and the death arc.

Then there is the grace note. PCs who died and are returned at the Source in this tier come back whole — not Touched, not diminished. The Emberthrone is the one place the Dimming can be undone. After twenty levels of death-with-teeth, the campaign earns the right to give one back clean.

Each ending has its own epilogue prompt.
- **Old Order:** what does each PC bury, now that burying means something again?
- **Pyrrhic:** what does the long winter cost them, and who do they become in it?
- **Mercy:** what is it like to be a hero-lieutenant of a kind, grieving, world-ending god?
